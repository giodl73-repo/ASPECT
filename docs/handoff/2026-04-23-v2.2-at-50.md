## Key Discoveries at 50

**1. The ceiling question is answered.** The project high is 93.6 (Pythagorean Chu-pei proof). The gap from 100 is not rubric miscalibration — it is genuine incompleteness in any real work: some Integrity uncertainty, some Legibility friction, some Resonance limitation. Perfect works don't exist; 93 is the ceiling we can see from here.

**2. Original design can beat canonical.** The vaccines work (92.7) exceeds Beck Underground (91.1) for the first time in the project. A work designed from scratch with v2.2 in hand, on a well-matched subject with clean data, can outperform a 90-year-old founding canonical work. The rubric is now generative, not just critical.

**3. The three-Minard calibration confirmed the rubric's neutrality.** Napoleon (85.5), Hannibal (85.1), and Wine (84.4) are separated by at most 1.1 points on technical dimensions. Emotional weight does not inflate scores. The Napoleon map earns its score through data quality and dimensional completeness — not sentiment.

**4. Purpose is the most discriminating dimension.** The variance between works is highest in Purpose. A technically accomplished propaganda poster (0049) and the best scientific illustration in the project (0044b) are 36 points apart. The dimension correctly distributes: functional/demonstrative works cluster at 12-14; advocacy works cluster at 11-15 depending on stakes proportionality; manipulation-as-purpose lands below 5.

**5. The proof elegance finding.** The Chinese Pythagorean proof (0044b, 93.6) outscores Euclid's proof (0044a, 86.7) by 6.9 points. Both prove the same theorem. The gap is real and explained: the Chu-pei proof achieves its demonstration in one visual move (rearrangement) vs. Euclid's multi-step construction. Elegance of demonstration IS a scoring variable, not an aesthetic preference. Innovation #275.
