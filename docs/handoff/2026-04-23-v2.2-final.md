## The Three-Cycle Experiment — What We Learned

The experiment did not reach 92+. The current project high stands at 91.5 (Beck). The ceiling for statistical-graphics originals appears to be approximately 90–91 when data quality is the limiting factor. What did reach 90.6 in three cycles:

1. **Cycle 1 → Cycle 2 (+11.4 pts)**: The sort-order change was the entire gain. Matrix reordering revealed the income-tier pattern that alphabetical order concealed.
2. **Cycle 2 → Cycle 3 (+4.6 pts)**: Counterfactual range bands + companion scatter + mechanism annotations. Smaller gain, more work.

**The rubric worked as a design guide.** Starting with v2.1 in hand, each revision targeted a specific dimension. The biggest gains were in Purpose (school-story match confirmed from brief) and Integrity (counterfactual checklist from T cluster). This validates the rubric as a design tool, not just a criticism tool.

**The GLASS ceiling innovation (#270)** is the most important finding: some data quality constraints are irreducible properties of the surveillance landscape, not design failures. A visualization of global antibiotic resistance will always carry an Integrity dock because the WHO surveillance system doesn't have comparable data across all 195 countries. The rubric should note when a score ceiling is data-landscape-determined rather than design-determined.
