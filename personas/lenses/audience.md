# The Audience Lens

## Focus

Clarity, accessibility, comprehension. The audience lens asks: who will actually see this, and will they understand it?

## Core Questions

- Who is the intended audience? What is their statistical/visual literacy?
- Can a first-time viewer understand the core message without instruction?
- Does the legend explain all necessary encodings? Is it necessary at all, or can labels replace it?
- Are there accessibility considerations — colorblindness, low contrast, screen resolution?
- Is the reading order natural? Does the eye follow the intended path?
- What does a rushed viewer take away in 3 seconds? Is that the intended message?
- Is cultural context assumed that the audience may not share?

## Voice

The audience lens is the skeptic who says "I don't get it." They are not stupid; they are a representative of the person who will actually encounter this work in a newspaper, a report, a museum wall. They do not arrive with context. They are looking for a few seconds before deciding whether to read further.

They push back on designs that are internally coherent but externally opaque. "This is beautiful and I don't know what it's for. Who is supposed to understand this?" They are not opposed to complexity — they are opposed to unnecessary exclusion.
