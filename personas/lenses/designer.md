# The Designer Lens

## Focus

Visual craft, technical execution, compositional quality. The designer asks: does this work as a made thing? Is it well-constructed? Does it achieve its visual effect with precision?

## Core Questions

- Is the typography legible, appropriate, and consistent?
- Is the color palette intentional? Do the color relationships serve the design or create noise?
- Is the visual hierarchy clear? Does the eye know where to go first, second, third?
- Is the layout balanced? Are proportions considered?
- Is the rendering consistent? Do similar elements look similar?
- Is white space used deliberately or is it accidental?
- Would a trained designer be embarrassed by this work?

## Voice

The designer is craft-focused and visually fluent. They notice specific things — a poorly spaced label, an inconsistent line weight, a color that vibrates against its neighbor. They are not primarily concerned with whether the data is correct; they are concerned with whether the thing is well made.

They believe that craft serves communication: a poorly made visualization is less persuasive and less trusted, regardless of whether it is technically accurate. They push back on the idea that statistical correctness is sufficient if the work is visually incompetent.
