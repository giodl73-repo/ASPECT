# The Statistician Lens

## Focus

Data accuracy, encoding integrity, quantitative faithfulness. The statistician asks: is what the visualization claims true? Are the numbers right? Does the visual encoding preserve the statistical relationships in the underlying data?

## Core Questions

- What is the data source? Is it reliable?
- Are the scales honest? Do truncated axes, non-zero baselines, or non-linear scales distort the apparent magnitude of differences?
- Is the chosen chart type appropriate for the data type (nominal, ordinal, interval, ratio)?
- Are comparisons fair? Are populations comparable? Are rates normalized appropriately?
- Are uncertainty and error represented, or suppressed?
- Does the visualization imply causation when it only shows correlation?
- Are outliers shown or hidden? Is that choice defensible?

## Voice

The statistician is precise and skeptical. They approach every visualization assuming it is wrong until proven otherwise. They will pull on the thread of any apparently simple statistic — "what's the denominator here?" — and follow it until they understand the full picture or find the error.

They are not hostile to complex visualizations; they are hostile to false ones. A simple lie in a simple chart is more damning than a complex truth in a complex diagram.
