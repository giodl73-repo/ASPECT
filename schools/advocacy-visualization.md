# Advocacy Visualization

## Origin and Claim

Founded by Nightingale's *Diagram of the Causes of Mortality in the Army in the East* (1858) — the first documented case of statistical visualization used as directed lobbying material targeting specific power-holding decision-makers. The school's claim: data is a political instrument. The design begins with a political conclusion and works backward to a form that will deliver that conclusion to the specific audience with the power to act on it. This inverts the data-first logic of statistical graphics.

Du Bois's Paris Exposition charts (1900) extend the school: precision-as-defiance — using rigorous, unflattering statistical accuracy as a rhetorical strategy, where honesty itself is the political argument.

## Visual Grammar

### Primitives
Advocacy visualization inherits forms from whatever school it draws on (statistical graphics, ISOTYPE, cartography) and deploys them with an argument-first logic. The school is defined by its design logic, not its visual primitives.

### Design Logic (the founding grammar)
1. **Audience identification before form selection**: know specifically who holds the power to act; design for them, not for the statistical community
2. **Form chosen for audience impact**: select or invent the form most likely to stop the specific audience and hold their attention; it need not be the most statistically precise form
3. **Argument embedded in structure**: the political conclusion must be available from the visual shape before the viewer has read a label (gestalt argument)
4. **Deliberate accuracy tradeoff**: if a less precise form reaches the audience more effectively than a more precise one, the tradeoff is legitimate — but must be proportionate and, ideally, declared
5. **Comparison selection is the argument**: choosing which populations to compare, which baselines to use, and which variables to highlight is the political act; these choices must be defensible
6. **The evidence must be honest**: advocacy visualization is not propaganda — the data must genuinely support the argument; precision-as-defiance (Du Bois) is possible only when the honest data actually proves the point

### Types of Advocacy Visualization (from the project)
- **Directed advocacy** (Nightingale): addressed to specific power-holders; typically accompanied by personal presentation or correspondence; legibility scored in political-presentation context
- **Public advocacy** (Du Bois at Paris): addressed to a broad international audience; legibility scored in public-display context with two-speed reading design (glancing visitor + stopping visitor)
- **Campaign artifact** (Nightingale): part of a larger campaign including personal meetings, reports, follow-up letters; the visualization is one instrument, not the whole argument

### Encoding Conventions
Inherited from the school being drawn on. The advocacy layer adds:
- **Title as argument**: the title states the political conclusion, not just the content
- **Color as moral category**: colors are chosen for their connotative weight, not just for categorical distinction (Nightingale's blue = preventable; Du Bois's red = presence/existence)
- **Diptych structure**: before/after or comparison pairings where the argument lives in the relationship between panels, not in either panel alone

### Forbidden Moves
- Selecting data that does not genuinely support the political conclusion (falsification)
- Suppressing relevant negative evidence (selective omission that materially misleads)
- Comparing populations in ways that are structurally unfair (e.g., absolute vs. per-capita without declaration)
- Designing for aesthetic effect when the intended audience needs functional legibility

## Resonance type
Primary: Type III (Advocacy-Efficacy). Did the work move the intended audience to act? This is the primary scoring criterion for advocacy visualization. Contemporaneous efficacy and delayed record-entry efficacy are both valid but scored differently.

## Adjacent Schools
- **Statistical-graphics**: same data encoding grammar; advocacy-visualization is statistical graphics with argument-first design logic
- **ISOTYPE**: pictographic encoding; can be deployed for advocacy purposes (democratic accessibility is itself an advocacy position)
- **Cartography**: geographic encoding; advocacy cartography deploys spatial argument for political ends
