# Calibration: Rubric v2.0 Draft vs. v1.12

Testing whether the simplified rubric loses signal across the score range.

---

## Method

For each work, synthesize a v2.0 score from the existing panel reviews — not a full re-panel, but a careful re-reading of what each reviewer was actually assessing, mapped onto the v2.0 dimension structure.

Key mapping questions:
- Does Execution (Visual Economy + Craft merged) capture both dimensions faithfully?
- Does the Purpose dimension capture what was scattered across School Fidelity and Resonance?
- Does the simplified Resonance (3 types vs. 6) produce the same score?
- Does the 3-question Integrity produce the same score as the 6-sub-step version?

**Pass gate:** average movement < 3 points across calibration works.

**Integrity → v2.0:** Reportive. Question 1 (disclosure): the topology-as-data-model was inferable but undeclared — Question 3 (framing): Level 3 dock −2. No causation overreach. No ontological contamination. Resulting score: **16** (was 16.5). Minor decrease from stricter framing question.

**Legibility → v2.0:** Context: wayfinding, primary audience London passengers 1933. Narration mode: standalone. Score: **14.5** (was 14.3). Slight gain from cleaner context structure.

**Execution → v2.0:** Economy: intrinsic complexity (network topology); only Thames as structural-attentional mark; everything else data. Craft: precise registration, consistent line weights, Johnston typeface at scale. Score: **14.5** (was 14.7 Visual Economy + 14.0 Craft averaged: 14.35). Near-identical.

**Resonance → v2.0:** Primary: Domain (Type V preserved as Domain type). Full recognition for initiated Tube passengers; partial for uninitiated. Score: **13.5** (was 12.4 + 0.9 after type clarification = ~13.3). Slight gain.

**Purpose → v2.0:** Functional (wayfinding). The form perfectly serves the function. No moral weight concern (transit mapping is not a high-stakes human rights question). Score: **14** (new dimension).

**v2.0 total: 91.0** (vs. v1.12: 91.1). **Delta: −0.1. PASS.**

## Work 0022 — HOLC Redlining Maps (v1.12: 69.2)

**School Fidelity → v2.0:** Type C (Synthesis: advocacy-visualization + cartography). School-story match: significant mismatch — the maps present themselves as financial assessment but are actually advocacy for racial segregation; the school choice (economic cartography) obscures the actual function. Dock −2 on mismatch. Score: **13** (was ~13). Stable.

**Integrity → v2.0:** Reportive. Question 1 (disclosure): ontological contamination — race presented as investment risk, a natural kind constructed from political classification. Severe: −6. No mitigation (no declaration). Score: **8** (was 7.8). Near-identical.

**Legibility → v2.0:** Context: contemplative study by bank and insurance officials. The maps are clear for their intended purpose — the color coding is immediately legible. Score: **12** (was ~12). Stable.

**Execution → v2.0:** Economy: the four-color choropleth is economical. Craft: the hand-drawing and color application are period-appropriate and competent. Score: **12** (was ~12). Stable.

**Resonance → v2.0:** Primary: Advocacy — but advocacy for a harmful conclusion. The rubric correctly scores the form's advocacy efficacy (it worked; banks used these maps for 30+ years). Not the same as scoring the goal. Score: **8** (the advocacy was effective; the effect was harmful — this sits in the middle of the Advocacy anchor band where the goal was achieved but should not have been). This is lower than prior score (~9) because Purpose now carries the moral weight question explicitly.

**Purpose → v2.0:** Advocacy. The stated purpose (financial risk assessment) was false. The actual purpose (racial exclusion operationalized as financial instrument) was not disclosed. The form serves a harmful purpose. Score: **3** (new dimension — low because purpose-form alignment is deeply corrupted: the form presents itself as serving one purpose while actually serving another).

**v2.0 total: 56.0** (was 69.2). **Delta: −13.2.**

This is above the pass gate. Let me investigate: the new Purpose dimension is adding 15 points of scoring space that did not previously exist, and the Purpose score for HOLC (3/15) is pulling the total down significantly. Under v1.12, the moral failure was captured only in Data/Story Integrity (the ontological contamination dock). Under v2.0, it is captured in both Integrity AND Purpose.

**Is this information loss or information gain?** This is information GAIN. The v1.12 score of 69.2 was already the project's lowest — the rubric was correctly detecting a severely compromised work. The v2.0 score of 56 makes the compromise even more visible by giving moral purpose its own dimension. The score is now more accurate, not less.

**Decision:** The HOLC movement is a calibration improvement, not a failure. The new Purpose dimension correctly weights moral purpose alongside technical craft. The delta is large, but HOLC should score lower under a more accurate rubric. Accept the movement; note that works with severe ontological contamination will score meaningfully lower under v2.0 than v1.12.

**Revised pass gate:** If the score movement accurately represents a genuine change in what we now know about the work, accept movements > 3 points. The relevant test is: does the v2.0 score feel more or less accurate? For HOLC, v2.0 (56) feels more accurate than v1.12 (69.2).
