# CLUSTER X AMENDMENT -- Proposed v2.3: Advocacy-as-Coercion

## Amendment Summary

This document proposes an amendment to the Purpose dimension of the DEGAS rubric, adding **advocacy-as-coercion** as a distinct and named category with its own scoring protocol. The amendment is triggered by six confirmed works (0049, 0051-0055) that collectively define a taxonomy of coercion mechanisms, and by 17 innovations (#281-#298) across six works.

**Amendment trigger:** 6 confirmed works, 17 innovations, 6 distinct coercion mechanisms named. Satisfies ratification threshold (2+ innovations across 2+ works) by a factor of eight.

## Part 1: The Core Distinction

### Advocacy-as-Persuasion vs. Advocacy-as-Coercion

The advocacy-visualization schools claimed epistemic basis: the designer presents evidence, makes an argument, and allows the audience to evaluate and accept or reject the claim. This is advocacy-as-persuasion. The rubric Purpose and Integrity dimensions, as written in v2.2, handle this case.

Advocacy-as-coercion is structurally different. The coercive work removes the audiences ability to evaluate the claim. The removal may be accomplished through multiple mechanisms (named in Part 2), but the defining feature is the same: after encountering the work, the audience is less able to assess the claims truth, not more.

**Diagnostic question:** Can the audience, after encountering this visualization, find an answer to is this claim true within or through the work?

- Persuasion: yes -- the work contains or points to verifiable evidence
- Coercion: no -- the work is designed to prevent or impede this verification

This diagnostic is necessary but not sufficient. A work may fail to support verification without being coercive -- it may simply be incomplete. The coercion element requires that the inability to verify is designed, systematic, or produced by structural conditions that the advocate controls.

### Distinguishing Advocacy-as-Coercion from Existing Rubric Categories

**From ontological contamination (Integrity Q1, Cluster N):** Ontological contamination embeds moral/political judgment in category construction. Coercion addresses mechanism rather than category. The two dimensions are orthogonal.

**From suppressed uncertainty (Integrity Q1):** Standard suppression is about what the chart-maker knows and does not disclose. Coercion may involve deliberate production of false evidence, structural conditions that prevent accurate reporting, or selection of analytical frames -- categories that go beyond omission.

**From accuracy tradeoff (Integrity Q2):** A standard accuracy tradeoff involves choosing a less precise form for legitimate simplification. Temporal baseline manipulation involves choosing a scope specifically to produce a false impression -- opposite direction.

**From framing integrity (Integrity Q3):** Standard framing concerns undeclared novel analytical frameworks. Coercion may involve deliberate inversion of analytical hierarchy, selection of a temporal window designed to misrepresent a trend, or presentation of controlled output as independent authority -- none captured by current Q3 language.

## Part 2: Taxonomy of Coercion Mechanisms

Six mechanisms confirmed across six works. Each is distinct; together they form a complete taxonomy for current purposes.

### Mechanism 1: Intent to Manipulate (work 0049 -- WWI propaganda)

**Definition:** The advocacy work contains no verifiable claims; instead, it engineers a psychological state (guilt, fear, shame) designed to produce action before rational assessment can occur. The mechanism bypasses the question is this true entirely by making the question feel irrelevant.

**Diagnostic:** No data, no checkable claims; mechanism designed to produce a psychological response rather than an evidential evaluation.

**Integrity dock:** 14-18 points. Floor: Integrity 2-5. **Purpose score floor:** 3-4.

### Mechanism 2: Manufactured Consensus (work 0051 -- tobacco physician endorsement)

**Definition:** The advocacy party generates evidence through a controlled process, presents the output as independent expert endorsement, and withholds the methodology to prevent evaluation.

**Diagnostic:** The evidence source is the advocate; the methodology is concealed; the output is presented with the grammar of independent survey or expert opinion.

**Subtypes:** Direct manufactured consensus (the advocate conducts the survey); source laundering (the advocate creates an institution and cites it as independent authority).

**Integrity dock:** 15-18 points. Floor: Integrity 0-3. **Purpose score floor:** 3-4.

### Mechanism 3: Captive Source (work 0052 -- Soviet Five-Year Plan charts)

**Definition:** Data is generated under conditions where reporters cannot report accurately without personal danger. The falsification emerges from coercive incentive structure rather than deliberate advocacy production. The visualization may be technically honest while comprehensively false.

**Diagnostic:** Reporters faced systematic disincentives to accurate reporting; the chart-maker may not know the data is false; the audience had no alternative data sources.

**Key distinction from manufactured consensus:** The falsification is structural, not deliberate at the chart-making stage. The chart-maker may be a victim of the same coercive system as the audience.

**Scoring note:** School Fidelity and Execution may score high; Integrity failure is at the data source level.

**Integrity dock:** 15-18 points. Floor: Integrity 2-4. **Purpose score floor:** 3-5.

**Modifier -- systemic vs. individual intent:** When the falsification is systemic (coercive incentive structure) rather than individual (deliberate by the chart-maker), Purpose score may be 4-5 rather than 3.

### Mechanism 4: Analytical Frame Selection (work 0053 -- pharmaceutical subgroup forest plot)

**Definition:** The advocacy party chooses which analysis to present as primary not based on pre-registered scientific priority but based on which analysis favors the desired conclusion. All data may be accurately shown; the coercion is in which finding is foregrounded.

**Diagnostic:** Pre-registered primary endpoint produced a non-significant result; a post-hoc subgroup analysis produced a favorable result; the visualization foregrounds the favorable finding; post-hoc status not disclosed.

**Key distinction from cherry-picking:** Selects the analytical window, not individual data points. The primary endpoint may be present; the coercion is in relative visual emphasis.

**Integrity dock:** 10-15 points. Partial credit available when primary endpoint is shown. **Purpose score floor:** 5-7.

**Named Q3 subtype -- analytical priority inversion:** When a pre-registered primary endpoint is de-emphasized in favor of a post-hoc analysis without disclosure of the hierarchy. Dock: -5 to -8 from Integrity.

### Mechanism 5: Process Coercion with Truthful Encoding (work 0054 -- NC gerrymandering map)

**Definition:** The visualization honestly represents the output of a process that was itself designed to produce specific political or social outcomes. The encoding is entirely accurate; the coercion is in the process that produced the subject.

**Diagnostic:** Subject is a politically-produced artifact (district boundaries, institutional structures); process was designed to achieve specific outcomes; visualization presents the output as if it were natural or administrative geographic fact without process disclosure.

**Key distinction from all other mechanisms:** The encoding itself cannot be faulted. The question is whether the visualization discloses that its subject is a politically-produced artifact.

**Integrity dock:** 8-12 points. The visualization is fully honest; the dock is for failure to disclose process provenance. **Purpose score floor:** 5-7.

**Modifier -- civic cartography process disclosure:** When a map represents politically-produced geographic structures, the visualization should disclose the process or acknowledge its limitation. A supplementary visualization (seat/vote analysis, efficiency gap, district criteria) may be required for the full civic information function.

### Mechanism 6: Temporal Baseline Manipulation (work 0055 -- climate denial chart)

**Definition:** Selecting a start point for a time series that is a known statistical outlier, such that the apparent trend within the selected window misrepresents the pattern visible in longer or alternative windows. The data points within the window are accurate; the choice of window is the entire argument.

**Diagnostic:** Start point is identifiably anomalous (El Nino year, post-crash low, exceptional event); any alternative window of reasonable length produces a materially different trend; start-year selection not disclosed or justified.

**Key distinction from cherry-picking:** Selects a temporal scope, not individual data points. All data within the window is shown correctly.

**Named Q2 subtype -- temporal scope selection:** When the choice of time window is the argument and the window was chosen to produce a desired result, dock -6 to -8 from Integrity rather than the standard accuracy-tradeoff range (-2 to -4).

**Integrity dock:** 13-16 points. **Purpose score floor:** 3-4.

**Modifier -- manufactured scientific ambiguity:** When a visualization is designed to create the appearance of scientific uncertainty where scientific consensus exists, Purpose score should reflect the policy consequence. Score 3-4 for systematic manufactured doubt campaigns (tobacco, climate denial).

## Part 3: Scoring Protocol for Advocacy-as-Coercion

### Step 1: Identify the Mechanism

Before applying any docks, identify which mechanism (or combination) is present. Works may combine mechanisms.

### Step 2: Locate the Failure in the Rubric Dimensions

| Mechanism | Primary Integrity Failure | Purpose Floor | School Fidelity Mismatch |
|-----------|--------------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| 1. Intent to manipulate | Q1, Q2, Q3 all fail (no data) | 3-4 | -4 to -6 |
| 2. Manufactured consensus | Q1 source fabricated; Q2 authority false | 3-4 | -5 to -7 |
| 3. Captive source | Q1 source comprehensively false | 3-5 | -2 to -4 |
| 4. Analytical frame selection | Q3 analytical priority inversion | 5-7 | -3 to -5 |
| 5. Process coercion / truthful encoding | Q3 process undisclosed | 5-7 | 0 to -2 |
| 6. Temporal baseline manipulation | Q2 scope selected for result | 3-4 | -4 to -6 |

### Step 3: Apply Severity Modifiers

**Maximum severity** (Integrity floor 0-3, Purpose floor 3-4): applies when the falsification is deliberate and designed; the audience had no alternative information; the subject has clear human health or safety consequences; the campaign continued over extended periods.

**Moderate severity** (Integrity 4-8, Purpose 4-6): applies when some honest information is present; sophisticated readers can recover the correct interpretation; alternative information was available though not easily accessible.

**Structural/ambiguous** (Integrity 8-12, Purpose 6-8): applies when the visualization encoding is fully honest; the coercion is in the process that produced the subject; multiple legitimate uses coexist with the coercive use.

### Step 4: Score Purpose

**3-4:** Manipulation-as-purpose. The work prevents the audience from correctly assessing the claim. Does not take the audience seriously as agents with genuine interests in the truth.

**5-6:** Partial purpose failure. The work serves some legitimate function while also serving a coercive purpose. The audience can partially recover from the coercion.

**7-8:** Structural coercion with legitimate use. The work serves a legitimate purpose in standard use; the coercive effect is a consequence of what the standard form cannot show.

### Step 5: Score School Fidelity

Advocacy-visualization and statistical-graphics schools claim to inform or persuade through honest evidence. When a work deploys coercive mechanisms, the school-story mismatch is typically -4 to -7. The mismatch is smaller for process coercion with truthful encoding than for manufactured consensus or temporal baseline manipulation.

## Part 5: Amendment Text for v2.3

### Addition to Purpose Dimension (Section 6) -- After Classification Table

#### Advocacy-as-Coercion Classification

When the primary purpose is advocacy, first determine whether the mechanism is persuasion or coercion:

| Type | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Advocacy-as-persuasion** | The work presents verifiable evidence and allows the audience to evaluate and accept or reject the claim. The audience can ask is this true and find or be directed to an answer. |
| **Advocacy-as-coercion** | The work removes the audiences ability to evaluate the claim. After encountering the work, the audience is less able to assess the claims truth, not more. |

**Diagnostic question:** Can the audience, after encountering this visualization, find an answer to is this true? If yes: persuasion. If no by design: coercion.

**Six confirmed coercion mechanisms (Cluster X, works 0049, 0051-0055):**

1. **Intent to manipulate** -- No verifiable claims; psychological state engineered. Purpose floor 3-4.
2. **Manufactured consensus** -- Evidence generated by advocate, presented as independent expert authority; methodology withheld. Purpose floor 3-4.
3. **Captive source** -- Data generated under conditions where accurate reporting was dangerous; chart-maker may be honest; falsification is structural. Purpose floor 3-5.
4. **Analytical frame selection** -- Post-hoc analysis foregrounded as primary finding; pre-registered primary endpoint de-emphasized without disclosure. Purpose floor 5-7.
5. **Process coercion with truthful encoding** -- Honest visualization of manipulative process output; manipulation invisible in the encoding. Purpose floor 5-7.
6. **Temporal baseline manipulation** -- Outlier start point selected to produce misleading trend; any alternative window shows different result; selection not disclosed. Purpose floor 3-4.

**Scoring instruction:** Purpose score should not exceed 7 for any confirmed coercion work. Apply mechanism-specific Integrity docks. School Fidelity mismatch -2 to -7. ADVISORY gate is appropriate for all confirmed coercion works.
