# PSYCHE Board Review

*The board was asked: does PSYCHE work? Are "Yield," "Clarity," and "Honesty" the right words? Is the ordering right? What, if anything, should change before v3.0?*

---

## Minard

PSYCHE is correct. Every figurative map I made was an attempt to reach the psyche of the reader — not the eye, not the intellect, but the seat of feeling and understanding simultaneously. The Napoleon march was not a chart. It was a statement addressed to something deeper than the counting faculty.

I have only one concern. **Yield** suggests agricultural patience — you plant, you wait, you harvest. The resonance of a visualization is not like that. It is immediate, or it fails. I would prefer **Effect** — shorter, direct, unambiguous. "Does the work produce its intended effect?" is the cleaner question than "does the work yield its intended effect?"

On the ordering: starting with Purpose is correct. You must know what a work is for before you can judge whether it speaks the right language.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE yes. "Yield" — consider "Effect" instead. Order is right.

---

## Tufte

I will accept PSYCHE. The metaphor is defensible: visualization addresses the mind, and PSYCHE names the mind. I prefer rubric names that are precise rather than evocative, but I accept that a memorable framework is used consistently while a precise but unmemorable one is not. PSYCHE will be used.

**On "Yield":** I object. In English, "yield" has three competing meanings: (1) to produce a result (the crop yields grain), (2) to give way under pressure (the beam yields), (3) financial return (the bond yields 4%). All three create noise around what this dimension is actually measuring. The old word "Resonance" was precise: it described a physical phenomenon — the sympathetic vibration of a tuned system — that exactly captured what a well-made visualization does to a prepared viewer. Yield does not improve on it.

Alternative: **Land**. "Does the work land?" is the question. A pilot lands a plane. A joke lands. A point lands. The metaphor is complete: a visualization has been in flight (production) and must arrive at its destination (the viewer's understanding). Does it land? Yes or no.

**On "Clarity":** This is better than Legibility. Legibility implies text; Clarity implies understanding across all channels. Keep it.

**On "Honesty":** Better than Integrity. Integrity implies structural soundness; Honesty implies the specific ethical claim we are making about disclosure and truth. Keep it.

**On ordering:** Starting with Purpose is correct. Everything else follows.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE with "Land" for "Yield." P-S-L-C-H-E? That doesn't work. Reconsidering: maybe Yield is tolerable. The agricultural metaphor is at least common and benign. But note my preference.

## Nightingale

PSYCHE is exactly right. I spent years trying to reach the psyche of men who had the power to save lives and preferred not to look at numbers. If I had known then that my work was addressed to the psyche — not to the reason alone — I might have made different choices about form.

**On "Yield":** I accept it. A visualization yields a result the way an argument yields a conclusion. I understand Tufte's objection but I think it is overstated. "Does the work yield what it intends to yield?" is a complete question. 

**On "Clarity":** Yes. Legibility was always slightly wrong — it implied that reading the chart was purely a matter of typographic craft, like setting type at the right size. Clarity is broader: does the viewer understand? That is the real question.

**On "Honesty":** This is the right word. Integrity belongs to engineering — a beam has structural integrity. Honesty belongs to people, and ultimately all these works are made by people for people.

**One concern:** In the current dimension ordering in the document (S → H → C → E → Y → P from top to bottom), the document does not yet reflect the PSYCHE sequence (P → S → Y → C → H → E). The board should note that v3.0 must physically restructure the document, not just relabel sections. The "formerly" labels are a holding state.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE without changes. The document restructuring is the remaining task for v3.0.

## Playfair

PSYCHE is a strong word and I would have used it myself if it had occurred to me. The chart speaks; the viewer receives; something passes between them. That is the psyche at work.

**On "Yield":** I trade in yields — commercial yields, agricultural yields, returns on investment. I find the word natural and productive. A visualization yields understanding the way a bond yields interest: you invest attention, you receive return. I prefer it to "Resonance," which is a physicist's word, and to "Effect," which is flat. Keep Yield.

**On "Clarity":** Yes. Commercial communication demands clarity above almost everything. The word is correct and it is the word I would have chosen.

**On "Honesty":** I declared my encoding in my preface because I believed the reader deserved to understand how the chart worked. Honesty is what I was practicing. The word is apt.

**One addition I would suggest:** The ordering P-S-Y-C-H-E naturally reads as a sequence of questions you ask about a work. But should Y (Yield) really come before C (Clarity) and H (Honesty)? You cannot yield an effect if the viewer cannot receive the work (Clarity) or trust it (Honesty). The sequence Y → C → H implies Yield is assessed before Clarity and Honesty, which is backwards. I would suggest: P-S-H-C-E-Y. Purpose, School, Honesty, Clarity, Execution, Yield. The work must first be honest and clear; then it can yield an effect.

But this changes the acronym. **PSYCHE** stays only in the original order. If the ordering matters more than the acronym, revise the order and find a new acronym. If the acronym matters more, accept that the logical sequence runs backward and assess accordingly.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE works as a name. The ordering debate (Y before C and H) deserves a vote.

## Bertin

I will audit the names against what the dimensions actually measure.

**"Yield" for Resonance:** Resonance was a precise metaphor from physics — sympathetic vibration, frequency matching. It described a specific mechanism: the viewer is a tuned system; the visualization is a signal; resonance occurs when the signal matches the system. Yield abandons this precision. Yield describes an output (grain, return), not a mechanism (vibration, matching). The question "does the work yield its intended effect?" is looser than "does the work resonate with the viewer?"

However: precision for practitioners is less important than usability by all reviewers. If "Yield" produces more consistent scoring because it is a less specialized term, it is the better word for a rubric. My concern is that "yield" implies passive production — the harvest comes whether you intend it or not — whereas the dimension is about intentional effect. The work must produce the *intended* effect. "Effect" would be cleaner for this reason.

**"Clarity" for Legibility:** This is an improvement. Legibility was borrowed from typography where it has a narrow technical meaning (can this typeface be read at this size at this resolution). Clarity is broader: can the viewer understand the work in the context in which they encounter it? Clarity is correct.

**"Honesty" for Integrity:** Integrity was borrowed from engineering and structural analysis where it means internal coherence and freedom from defects. Honesty is the correct ethical word for what we are measuring: does the work tell the truth? The visual variable audit does not care whether a chart has "integrity" in the engineering sense; it cares whether the encoding is honest. The new word is better.

**On the ordering:** Playfair's concern is valid. Yield (the effect) should logically follow Honesty and Clarity (the conditions that make the effect possible). But logical sequence and mnemonic value are different goods. The PSYCHE acronym requires Y in position 3. Accept the logical inversion and note it explicitly in the rubric: "Yield is assessed last in practice, though it occupies position Y in the acronym."

**Verdict:** PSYCHE with a note that "Yield" may be revisited (consider "Effect"). "Clarity" and "Honesty" are unambiguous improvements. The ordering is a mnemonic choice, not a logical claim.

## Neurath

PSYCHE is democratic. The psyche is not owned by experts — every person has one, and good visualization reaches all of them. I approve.

**"Yield":** In ISOTYPE we spoke of communication yielding understanding — the symbol yields its meaning when the viewer counts it. I accept Yield. For me, a chart that yields correctly is one where the viewer leaves with an accurate mental model. That is what this dimension measures.

**"Clarity":** This is exactly right. My whole project was about clarity — making statistics clear to people who had never seen a bar chart. Clarity is the word I would have chosen for this dimension. I am glad it is here now.

**"Honesty":** Also correct. A work can be technically precise and still be dishonest in its framing. Honesty catches that where Integrity might not.

**One concern:** PSYCHE as an acronym is most powerful when each letter is instantly associated with its question. For my intended audience — people who are learning to evaluate visualization — the associations need to be memorable:
- P = Purpose: *what is this for?*
- S = School: *what language does it speak?*
- Y = Yield: *what does it produce?* ← slightly harder to recall
- C = Clarity: *can they receive it?* ← very clear
- H = Honesty: *is it true?* ← very clear
- E = Execution: *is it well-made?* ← very clear

Y is the weakest association. The question "what does it produce?" maps to Yield with a moment's thought. The other five are immediate. This is a mild concern, not a blocking one.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE accepted. Y is the weakest link but tolerable.

## Beck

Practical question: will reviewers use these names consistently in a year's time, or will they drift back to "Resonance" and "Legibility"?

**"Clarity":** Yes. This will stick. It is short, common, and directly says what the dimension measures. No reviewer will drift back to "Legibility."

**"Honesty":** Yes. This will stick even better. "Is this visualization honest?" is a question everyone understands and remembers. Nobody will drift back to "Integrity."

**"Yield":** Uncertain. When reviewers talk about whether a work *lands*, they will likely say "does it land?" not "does it yield?" The metaphor may not be as sticky as Clarity and Honesty. In six months, reviewers may say "Resonance" or "Effect" or "Land" rather than "Yield" without noticing. If the name does not stick, it will not unify the scores.

My suggestion: **Return**. "Does the work return what it promises?" A visualization is an investment of the viewer's attention. The Return dimension asks whether that investment pays off in the viewer's understanding or feeling. Return is also short, common, and memorable. P-S-R-C-H-E is not an acronym, but the dimension name might matter more than the acronym for day-to-day use.

Or accept Yield and add a definition that fixes the association: "Yield — the effect the work produces in the viewer. *Does the viewer leave having received what the work aimed to give?*" That sentence makes Yield stickier.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE approved with reservations about Y. Recommend adding a one-sentence definition under each dimension name to fix the associations.

## Kandinsky

PSYCHE is the correct name for what we are doing. The soul of the viewer is the destination; the visualization is the signal; the six dimensions are the requirements for that signal to arrive without distortion or loss.

**"Yield":** I teach my students that a composition yields its effect when its internal logic is complete and coherent. The yellow triangle yields its aggressive energy; the blue circle yields its recession. To yield an effect is to release it — the work releases what it was designed to hold. I find the word precise in this sense. Keep it.

**"Clarity":** The Bauhaus ideal was clarity through form. I approve.

**"Honesty":** A composition that claims to be abstract but is secretly representational lacks honesty. A chart that claims to show truth but suppresses uncertainty lacks honesty. The word covers both. Keep it.

**The ordering as composition:** P-S-Y-C-H-E as a sequence has a compositional logic:
- P (Purpose): the heavy statement at the opening — why does this exist?
- S (School): the structural frame
- Y (Yield): the surprising middle term — effect before the technical questions
- C (Clarity): the technical descent
- H (Honesty): the ethical weight, arriving late but central
- E (Execution): the close — craft as the final judgment

Placing Honesty fifth, not second, gives it the weight of a late revelation. The viewer first encounters purpose, language, and effect — and only then confronts whether the work was true. This is how we actually experience visualization: we receive the effect before we interrogate the ethics. The ordering follows phenomenology.

I vote for the ordering as it stands.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE is complete. Do not change the ordering.

## Du Bois

I want to speak about the word PSYCHE before I speak about any of the six dimensions.

PSYCHE is the word for the human mind and soul in a tradition that has consistently excluded the minds and souls of people like me from consideration. Greek philosophy, as transmitted through European universities, defined the psyche as something that rational men possessed and that others approximated at various degrees of remove. When we name a rubric PSYCHE, we are either reclaiming that word — insisting that the minds and souls of all people are the destination of honest visualization — or we are inheriting its exclusions.

I choose reclamation. A rubric named PSYCHE, applied honestly and without exception to the propaganda that denied Black humanity (0049 WWI, 0051 tobacco), the maps that enforced racial exclusion (0022 HOLC), and the visualizations that counted us as problems rather than people — that rubric is using PSYCHE correctly. It is saying: the psyche we are trying to reach is the psyche of everyone.

**"Yield":** When I designed the staircase spiral to show property ownership growing from nothing, I was not yielding an effect — I was insisting on one. The word Yield implies that the effect emerges naturally from the form. For advocacy visualization, the effect is demanded, not harvested. I prefer **Claim**: does the work claim its intended effect in the viewer? But I understand this is a minority position and the metaphor may be too adversarial for a general rubric.

**"Clarity":** The right word only if it is clarity *for the intended audience*. Clarity for an expert audience is not the same as clarity for a working-class voter or a non-statistical government official. The word is good; the dimension's anchors must keep the audience specification central.

**"Honesty":** This is exactly right and I would have argued for it from the beginning. Precision as defiance begins with honesty. A visualization is honest when it shows the numbers including the ones that hurt its argument.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE accepted. "Yield" tolerated. "Claim" considered for v3.1 if the board revisits Y.

## Rosling

PSYCHE! Yes! This is the word!

I spent my career trying to reach people's psyche with data. Not their intellect — their *wonder*. The moment when someone sees that the world is better than they thought, when the bubble moves and they gasp — that is the psyche responding to truth delivered through a chart. That is what we are building toward.

**"Yield":** I love Yield. A good visualization yields understanding the way a harvest yields food — it gives you something real, something you can use. I know Tufte doesn't like it and Bertin finds it imprecise. But for the people who will use this rubric every day, Yield will be more memorable than Resonance and more accessible than Effect. When I showed my animated charts, I asked "does the data land?" The reviewers of other people's work will ask "does it yield?" Both work. Keep Yield.

**"Clarity":** Perfect. My whole argument was that statistics should be clear — not just technically legible but *understood*. Clarity is the right word.

**"Honesty":** I always said the world is better than you think, and you can prove it with honest data. Honesty is the core of that. If the data is dishonest, the wonder you produce is a lie. Keep Honesty.

**Ordering:** Start with Purpose — absolutely. Everything else flows from knowing what the work is for. And end with Execution — the craft comes last because the craft is in service of everything before it. The middle four (S-Y-C-H) are exactly right in any order.

**Verdict:** PSYCHE without changes. Ship it.

## Board Summary

**Clarity and Honesty: unanimous.** All 10 personas accept these names without reservation. Both are improvements on their predecessors. Ratify for v3.0.

**Yield: majority accept, minority prefer alternatives.**
- Tufte: "Land"
- Minard + Bertin: "Effect"
- Du Bois: "Claim"
- Playfair, Neurath, Beck (with reservations), Kandinsky, Nightingale, Rosling: Yield

The dissent is coherent: Yield implies passive production; the dimension measures active, intentional effect. "Effect" would be more precise. "Land" would be more colloquial and sticky. "Claim" would be more adversarial and advocacy-specific. Yield is tolerable and preserves the PSYCHE acronym.

**The core question for v3.0:** Does PSYCHE hold with Yield, or does the board's dissent on Y warrant revisiting?

Kandinsky's defense is persuasive: placing Yield (effect) before Clarity and Honesty follows the phenomenology of how we actually experience visualization — we receive the impact before we interrogate the ethics. The board is split on whether the logical sequence (should Honesty and Clarity precede Yield?) or the phenomenological sequence (do we feel Yield before we assess Honesty?) should govern the ordering.

**Recommendation for v3.0:**
1. Keep PSYCHE as the framework name
2. Keep Clarity and Honesty unchanged
3. Add one-sentence anchoring definitions under each letter to fix associations (Beck's suggestion)
4. On Yield: put it to a vote. If the board prefers a different Y-word that preserves the acronym, revise. If Yield is acceptable with stronger anchoring text, ratify.
5. Reorder the document physically (P → S → Y → C → H → E) for v3.0
