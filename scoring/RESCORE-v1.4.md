**Data/Story Integrity — Step D assessment:**

Snow's analytical framework: *spatial clustering of events around a candidate source constitutes causal evidence for that source's role.* In 1854, this framework did not exist as an established method. Snow was proposing it by deploying it — using it as if it were valid methodology, rather than declaring "I am proposing a new method of source identification." The map presents deaths at their locations and trusts the viewer to supply the clustering-as-causation premise. In 1854, many viewers (including the General Board of Health) did not supply this premise; they saw the same map and attributed the clustering to local miasma.

Step D: novel, undeclared, non-obvious in 1854 (the analytical framework was the scientific innovation itself). Dock −2 to −3. Applying −2 (the negative evidence — brewery, workhouse — partially bridges the gap by strengthening the framework's plausibility for contemporary readers; but the framework remains undeclared).

## Reading the Amended Rubric's Effect

**Step D penalizes works that carry their intellectual innovation silently.** Beck and Snow both built their strongest contribution into a form that enacts but doesn't declare the new data model. The penalty is proportional: Beck's topology is more visible (inferable, −1), Snow's clustering-as-causation is less visible (non-obvious in its time, −2). The rubric now rewards declaration: if you invent a new framework, say so.

**Type V lifts scores that were previously awkward to score.** Beck and Snow both had Resonance scores in the 12–13 range under v1.3 because the undifferentiated anchors were a poor fit for domain-gated resonance. Under v1.4, both gain about 1 point. The gain is real: these maps do something exceptional for the initiated viewer that the general anchors couldn't capture.

**The net of the two amendments is near-zero for Beck (−0.1) and slightly negative for Snow (−1.2).** This is correct: both maps have a genuine flaw (undeclared framework) that partially offsets a genuine strength (domain-gated resonance). The rubric is now more honest about both.

**Works not touched by either amendment (0001, 0002, 0004) are stable across v1.0–v1.4.** Minard's slow decline (86.1 → 83.5) is entirely from Data/Story Integrity amendments that correctly identified confidence suppression and implied causation. Nightingale's rise (78.8 → 81.2) is entirely from amendments that recognized her intentional design choices (polar form, advocacy context) rather than penalizing them as failures.
