## What v1.5 did

Every work except Minard (Type C, no H/J surface) and Snow (no applicable amendment) improved. The improvements are not cosmetic — each reflects a genuine correction:

**Haeckel's rise (+3.0)** is the largest single move in the project's history. The rubric was penalizing biological illustration for accurately representing intrinsically complex organisms. H fixed that. Haeckel was at the bottom of the board (81.1); he is now in the middle (84.1), which is where a work with unanimous 15/15 Craft and real domain-gated resonance belongs.

**Playfair's rise (+1.0)** rewards the only founder who declared his grammar at the moment of invention. The declaration is not just good documentation — it is why the bar chart became universal. You cannot spread what you cannot teach.

**Neurath's rise (+1.1)** resolves the Tufte vs. Neurath disagreement: the rubric now recognizes that visual economy is relative to the audience's cognitive baseline, not absolute. Repeated pictograms are more economical than bar charts for the population they were designed to reach.

**Snow does not move.** His undeclared non-obvious framework still incurs the largest Step D penalty in the project. The gradation does not reduce it; it confirms it. Snow was right; he just didn't say why.
