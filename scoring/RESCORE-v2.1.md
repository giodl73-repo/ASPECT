## 4. Summary Findings

### What Moved Most, and Why

Works that moved more than 2 points in either direction:

**Upward movers:**
- 0001 Minard: +2.5 (Purpose credit for synthesis serving narrative)
- 0006 Haeckel: +2.4 (Execution averaging more favorable; intrinsic complexity correctly weighted)
- 0021 Rosling: +2.5 (Purpose 14 for advocacy+wonder alignment)

**Downward movers:**
- 0013 Schematic Network: -4.4 (Purpose absorbs school-story mismatch fully)
- 0017 Booth: -6.7 (Purpose captures partial moral concealment in categories)
- 0022 HOLC: -14.2 (Purpose 3 for active purpose falsification — accuracy gain)

### What the Consistent Scoreboard Reveals

**Finding 1: The Purpose dimension redistributes credit and blame more accurately.**

The six works that moved most are not random. They cluster into two groups: gainers where form-purpose alignment was strong but uncredited (Rosling, Minard, Haeckel), and losers where purpose failure was distributed weakly across other dimensions (Booth, Schematic Network, HOLC). Purpose gives these patterns a dedicated home.

**Finding 2: The Purpose dimension creates a new separation at the bottom.**

v1.x bottom five by score: 0022 HOLC (69.2), 0013 Schematic (76.4), 0017 Booth (78.7), 0012 Isotype-Plastic (79.4), 0011 Urban Heat (80.8).

v2.1 bottom five: 0022 HOLC (55), 0013 Schematic (72), 0017 Booth (72), 0011 Urban Heat (78), 0012 Isotype-Plastic (78.5).

HOLC is now dramatically separated from the rest of the scoreboard — 17 points below the second-worst work rather than 7 points. This is correct: HOLC is categorically different from the other low scorers. It is not merely a technically imperfect work; it is a work that falsified its stated purpose to enact racial harm.

**Finding 3: The range widens appropriately.**

v1.x range: 69.2 to 91.1 = 21.9 points.
v2.1 range: 55 to 91.5 = 36.5 points.

The widening is signal-improvement, not score drift. Works that succeed at purpose stay where they were; works that fail at purpose move distinctly lower. The rubric now stretches further at the bottom to accommodate the full range of purpose-form misalignment, from mismatch to concealment to falsification.

**Finding 4: Execution averaging flattens significant outliers.**

Works where Visual Economy and Craft diverged by 3+ points (0015 Kandinsky Waiting: 13 vs 17; 0007 Neurath: 14 vs 12) lose diagnostic clarity under the averaged Execution score. The merge was the right simplification decision, but reviewers should retain the habit of noting large Economy-Craft divergences in prose in panel SUMMARYs.

**Finding 5: Integrity is the most stable dimension.**

The consolidation from six sub-steps to three questions produced no movements larger than 2 points in Integrity scores for any work except those where the demonstrative/reportive routing changed. This confirms that the three-question structure captures the same signal at lower reviewer overhead.

**Finding 6: The consistent scoreboard makes a structural argument.**

The incremental v1.x scoring approach was productive for discovering the rubric's structure. But it produced a scoreboard not fully comparable across works. The v2.1 rescore creates the first fully consistent scoreboard for works 0001-0025.

The key pattern: works that succeed at being what they claim AND at serving a genuine human need score 85-91. Works that succeed technically but fail at purpose score in the 70s. Works that actively falsify their stated purpose (HOLC: 55) are appropriately isolated at the bottom of the distribution.

This is what a calibrated rubric looks like: the score reflects the work's relationship to its audience and subject, not only its technical craft.
