## 2. Dimension Analysis

### School Fidelity /20

- Mean: approximately 16.4
- Range: 13 (0013 schematic-science-network, 0022 HOLC) to 19 (0003 Beck, 0009 Playfair)
- Hardest to score above 18: requires Type B founder with declared grammar (Cluster J bonus) or Type A with completely clean application. Only 4 of 33 works reach 18+.
- Most contested: 0022 HOLC (Type C synthesis under redlining context); 0013 (geographic anchor ambiguity before v1.9)

Above 18 effectively requires founders who explicitly declared their grammar — Beck's Underground map principles are self-evident in the map's regularity; Playfair's axes are explained in his Atlas preface. Type A members are naturally capped around 16-18 because small grammar lapses are always detectable.

### Data/Story Integrity /20

- Mean: approximately 15.8
- Range: 8 (0022 HOLC, Cluster N Step F dock) to 18 (0029 child mortality)
- Hardest to score above 17: requires perfect source declaration, no causation overreach, correct variable selection, and no ontological contamination.
- Most contested: 0022 HOLC (Cluster N dock created the largest single reviewer spread in the project)

The Cluster N amendment (Step F, ontological category integrity) was the most consequential single amendment. HOLC's race-as-risk-proxy contamination produced a dock of 5-7 points on DSI alone. This is the rubric's strongest statement about the relationship between visualization and injustice.

### Legibility /15

- Mean: approximately 12.8
- Range: 9.9 (0026 data-art) to 15 (0003 Beck — perfect wayfinding score)
- Hardest to score above 13: works with complex overlapping encodings or context decay
- Most contested: context specification disagreements (what is the primary reading context for historically displaced works?)

The Cluster D amendment (v1.3, context specification) resolved most legibility scoring disagreements by anchoring scores to a declared primary context. Works designed for wayfinding (0003) score highest because wayfinding's criterion (task completion) is easily met by a well-designed transit map.

### Visual Economy /15

- Mean: approximately 12.9
- Range: 11 (0002 Nightingale coxcomb early scoring; 0026 data-art at Tufte's hand) to 15 (0025 Tufte VDQI)
- Hardest to score above 14: abstract, data-art, and intrinsic-complexity works where the economy criterion requires reinterpretation
- Most contested: 0026 data-art — Tufte 9, Kandinsky 14 — largest single-dimension reviewer spread in the project (5 points)

Visual Economy is where the rubric's founding tension (Tufte's efficiency maximization vs. the other schools' different criteria) is most visible. Clusters G and H resolved most cases; data art remains the active calibration gap (candidate Cluster Q).

### Resonance /15

- Mean: approximately 13.3
- Range: 11 (0011, 0013) to 15 (0033 coral reef, 0006 Haeckel — intrinsic beauty)
- Hardest to score above 14: requires full achievement of primary resonance type plus secondary type bonus
- Most contested: Type VI delayed resonance mechanism matrix — the politically-constructed delay bonus (+1 for Du Bois) is the only point-level add-on in the rubric and its calibration is uncertain

Resonance is the dimension with the most theoretical complexity (6 types, mechanism matrix, dynamic variable note). Despite this, reviewer spread is typically 2-3 points — lower than Visual Economy's contested cases.

### Craft /15

- Mean: approximately 13.0
- Range: 11 (0007 Neurath printing limitations; 0018 Euclid reproduction quality) to 17 (0015 Kandinsky Waiting — full control of medium)
- Hardest to score above 14: historical canonical works where reproduction quality varies by edition
- Most contested: least contested dimension — spread typically 1-2 points

Craft is the rubric's most stable dimension. The institutional-divergence note in Legibility's context specification has partially addressed the edition-quality issue for canonical imports.
