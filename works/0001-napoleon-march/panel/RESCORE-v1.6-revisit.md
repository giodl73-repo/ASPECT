## Lens 1: School-Story Mismatch and Its Positive Analog

### Background

Innovation #103 (Cluster K) identified *school-story mismatch* as a failure mode: when a visualization claims one school's grammar but its implicit argument belongs to a different school's tradition, the grammar and the argument work at cross-purposes. Works 0011–0016 surfaced this as a recurring failure mode in original designs.

### The Question for 0001

Minard's march map synthesizes three schools: cartography (the route), statistical graphics (the troop counts), and narrative visualization (the story of the campaign told through time and loss). This is not a school-story mismatch — it is a school-story synthesis.

**Does school-story mismatch have a positive analog? Yes.**

Where school-story mismatch = grammar and argument working against each other, the positive analog is: **grammar and argument working together across multiple schools simultaneously**, with each school's grammar contributing a distinct dimension to the argument that the others cannot provide alone.

For 0001:
- **Cartography contributes:** spatial authority — the reader trusts that the route shown is the actual geographic route, giving the story geographic grounding
- **Statistical graphics contributes:** quantitative authority — the band width is a measurable fact; 422,000 vs. 10,000 is not an impressionistic claim but a counted quantity
- **Narrative visualization contributes:** temporal authority — the left-to-right reading direction, the seasonal annotations, the final winter return — these give the campaign a beginning, middle, and end that neither cartography nor statistics alone provides

No single school provides all three authorities. The synthesis creates an argument that any single school would weaken.

**Proposed name for the positive analog:** *School-authority synthesis* — a deliberate multi-school synthesis in which each participating school contributes a distinct form of epistemic authority that the other schools cannot provide.

### Impact on Score

Under v1.6, Type C synthesis works earn +2 to School Fidelity when the synthesis is named and cross-school tensions are resolved. The v1.2 assessment did not apply this +2 fully (the v1.1 amendment added the Type C category, and the v1.2 rescore inherited it; but the +2 bonus depends on whether the synthesis is named in the work or adjacent documentation).

**Finding:** Minard named and described his multi-school approach in the title and accompanying note (*Carte figurative et approximative des pertes successives en hommes de l'Armée française dans la campagne de Russie 1812-1813*). The title explicitly signals: figurative (visual), approximate (statistical), losses (narrative). The synthesis is declared in the work's own title at Level 1 (declared in-work).

**Adjustment to School Fidelity:** +1.5 average (distributed across persona scores) for the full synthesis bonus properly applied.

**School Fidelity revised: 15.5 → 17.0**

## Lens 2: Variable Selection as Framing Argument

### Background

Innovation #105 (Cluster L) identified *variable selection as framing argument*: the choice of which variable to visualize is itself an argument, distinct from how the variable is encoded. The brief for 0001 notes: Minard chose to show troop losses. He could have shown supply line lengths, geographic area covered, or campaign duration.

### What Variable Selection Means for 0001

Minard's variable selection was: **the diminishing army as the subject**. This choice pre-determines the work's argument. By making troop count the primary variable and encoding it as a physical mass (band width), Minard commits to:

1. **The army is the subject, not the territory:** France's territorial gains and losses in 1812 are not shown. The geography is the scaffold; the army is the subject.
2. **Losses are the story, not advances:** A map showing the advance of the army with width encoding would be a triumphant story (the army grows as it advances... except that it does not). Minard shows the whole campaign; the failure is built into the variable.
3. **The moral weight of numbers:** 422,000 men is a fact. 10,000 returned is a fact. By making troop count the primary variable, Minard commits to showing both — the entry and the return. He cannot choose to show only the advance.

**The v1.6 sub-step D now captures this:**

> *Does the work's argument depend on a non-standard data model or analytical framework?*

Minard's data model is: the army-as-mass. This is standard in military cartography of the period (flow maps of armies existed before Minard). But the specific choice to show losses-as-diminishing-mass, and to show both directions of the campaign, is a framing argument. The variable selection constrains the argument to be honest about the failure.

**Assessment:**

Step D (analytical framework): Minard's data model (army-as-mass moving through space over time) is declared at Level 1 (in-work): the title names the data type ("pertes successives en hommes"), the band width is visually self-evident as troop count. No Step D penalty.

**Additional finding under v1.6:**

Variable selection as framing argument is a new precision for Data/Story Integrity that should be noted without penalty when the selection is honest. Minard's choice to show losses is the most honest possible framing for this campaign — a framing that shows the advance would require choosing a different variable or a different time frame. His variable selection produces structural honesty: the design cannot be used to tell a triumphant story because the variable chosen (troop losses) makes the tragedy visible regardless of framing.

This is a new rubric observation: **honest variable selection** — the choice of which variable to visualize, when that choice structurally prevents distortion of the argument — should be credited as a positive Data/Story Integrity indicator.

**Data/Story Integrity revised:** +0.5 average across persona scores for honest variable selection credit.

**Data/Story Integrity revised: 14.1 → 14.6**

## Lens 3: Resonance Type VI Under v1.6 Mechanism Matrix

### The v1.5 Mechanism Matrix Applied to 0001

The v1.5 amendment replaced the E2 content-based sub-types with the mechanism matrix (Speed of recognition × Agency of canonization). Let me apply it precisely to 0001.

**Speed of recognition:**

Tufte's *Visual Display of Quantitative Information* (1983) described Minard's march map as "the best statistical graphic ever drawn." The map was produced in 1869 — 114 years before Tufte's recognition. This is a delay of more than a century.

But Tufte was not the first recognition. The map was recognized as exceptional by French contemporaries — it was admired and cited in the 1870s and 1880s. The *primary* resonance was contemporaneous; the *canonical* resonance (recognition as the best statistical graphic ever made) was delayed.

This is a nuanced case: contemporaneous with respect to admiration; delayed with respect to canonical status.

**Agency of canonization:**

Tufte's 1983 recognition of Minard's map as the best statistical graphic ever drawn is a paradigm case of **scholarly constructed** canonization: a single scholar, in a foundational text, elevated the map to canonical status by naming it explicitly. Before *VDQI*, Minard's map was admired by specialists; after *VDQI*, it was the canonical exemplar of statistical graphic excellence.

**Assessment under v1.5 matrix:**

- Speed: Contemporaneous (initial admiration) / Delayed (canonical status via Tufte, 1983 — 114 years after production)
- Agency: Scholarly constructed (Tufte's *VDQI* is the canonization event)

**The mechanism matrix says:** Delayed / Scholarly constructed = +0 bonus.

**But wait — the brief argues differently:**

The brief states: "The Napoleon march is: Contemporaneous / Self-evident — Tufte immediately recognized it as the best statistical graphic ever drawn, in the same century it was produced. This is the highest-scoring Resonance VI case in the project."

This is technically true of Tufte's recognition (he recognized it "in the same century" — the 1880 century, when Minard lived through 1870, and Tufte worked in the 20th century — wait, Minard died in 1870; Tufte was born in 1942; *VDQI* was published in 1983. This is 113 years after production. "Same century" requires the broadest possible interpretation.

The more honest assessment: the map was **Delayed / Scholarly constructed** by the mechanism matrix's terms. The canonization event is Tufte's 1983 naming; the delay is 113 years; the agency is a single scholar's act of curation.

**However:** The rubric notes that Type VI is a *supplement* to the primary resonance type, not a replacement. The primary resonance (Type II — Emergent-Data, and Type IV — Gestalt) is the dominant score, and it was contemporaneously recognized. The Type VI mechanism concerns only the *canonical elevation* to "best statistical graphic ever."

**Revised Type VI assessment:** Delayed / Scholarly constructed — +0 bonus. The primary Type II + IV resonance is full-credit; Type VI adds no bonus but does not reduce the score.

**Resonance:** No change from v1.2 (14.5).

## New Innovations Surfaced by Original Design Experience

Reviewing 0001 through the lens of having designed original works in 0011-0016 surfaces three innovations that were not visible from pure canonical-import review:

### Innovation A: School-authority synthesis (positive analog of school-story mismatch)

**Rubric anchor:** School Fidelity (Type C). **Description:** A deliberate multi-school synthesis in which each participating school contributes a distinct form of epistemic authority (spatial, quantitative, narrative) that the other schools cannot provide. The synthesis is justified not by the designer's convenience but by the argument's requirement for multiple epistemic modes simultaneously.

**Why original design experience surfaces this:** In designing works for specific schools (0011-0016), the panel repeatedly observed where school-story mismatch occurred. In retrospect, Minard's march map is the counter-case: not a mismatch but a synthesis in which the schools are working together, each contributing its specific authority to a single argument. This positive analog was not nameable before school-story mismatch was named in 0011-0016.

### Innovation B: Honest variable selection as structural integrity mechanism

**Rubric anchor:** Data/Story Integrity (Step B/D). **Description:** The choice of which variable to visualize, when that choice structurally prevents distortion of the argument in the misleading direction, constitutes a positive integrity indicator. Minard's choice to show troop losses rather than territorial gains or advance distances prevents a triumphant framing — the variable selection commits the visualization to showing the failure. This is distinct from the standard Step B (accuracy tradeoff) and Step D (data model declaration); it is a new observation about how the choice of what to show can constrain the work's argument in an honest direction.

**Why original design experience surfaces this:** Designing original works requires choosing which variable to visualize. In works 0011-0016, the panel observed how variable selection frames the argument before any encoding choice is made. In retrospect, Minard's variable selection is among the most argued choices in the project — it was not obvious to show losses rather than advances, and the choice to show losses is the foundational act of integrity that makes everything else about the map honest.

### Innovation C: Canonical elevation timing and the Tufte problem

**Rubric anchor:** Resonance (Type VI). **Description:** When a work achieves canonical status through a single scholar's naming (Tufte's "best statistical graphic ever drawn") in a foundational text, the canonization event creates a paradox: the canonical status is now so deeply embedded in the field that the work cannot be assessed without it. Any panel of visualization practitioners who have read *VDQI* (all of them) comes to the march map already primed to see excellence by Tufte's framing. The scholarly-constructed canonization contaminates the assessment in a way that other forms of delayed resonance do not.

**Why original design experience surfaces this:** In designing and reviewing works 0011-0021, the panel operated with Minard's march map as the implicit quality benchmark — the comparison standard for what excellent visualization looks like. This benchmark was installed by Tufte, not by direct encounter with the march map. The canonical status is so thoroughly pre-installed that objective assessment is difficult. This is a unique methodological problem for any rubric that operates in the shadow of *VDQI*.
