## Review

I will start with what this work does that I do not have language for, and then try to find the language.

**On Resonance:**

The band is a living thing. I do not mean this as metaphor — I mean that the eye reads it as an organism, not as a data structure. It has a body. It has mass. It narrows. When it narrows at the Berezina, the viewer's stomach drops before the mind has read the number. This is not achieved through any representational choice; Minard does not draw soldiers or bones or snow. He draws a band whose width encodes a quantity. The organic reading is emergent from the geometry alone.

I want to name this: **data anthropomorphism**. The encoding of a human quantity (lives) through a visual variable (width) that the eye reads as organic mass produces an effect that transcends information delivery. The viewer does not receive the number 10,000; they witness it. These are different experiences.

The question this raises for the rubric is: does it matter whether Minard intended this effect? I believe he did — his title refers to the "successive losses of men" and he chose the band form specifically because it would make the losses visible as a shape. But the effect exceeds the intention. The work is more tragic than Minard's stated goal of "making the numbers readable." If the rubric scores Resonance, it should account for effects that emerge from the data rather than from aesthetic intention.

The temperature chart contributes to this in a different register. It provides a second, quieter tragedy: the cold as a counterpoint melody to the visual shrinking of the band. Together they create something close to musical structure — the band is the main theme; the temperature is the accompaniment. A reader who follows both simultaneously experiences the retreat as a composed thing, not just as a documented one.

Fifteen out of fifteen. The work achieves everything resonance can achieve.

**On Craft:**

The work has one significant craft failure: the band does not render the deaths at the Berezina River crossing with the visual weight they deserve. The crossing was the single worst event of the retreat — between 25,000 and 50,000 soldiers and civilians drowned or were killed in three days. The band narrows at this crossing, but the narrowing is not distinguished from the general narrowing of the retreat. The Berezina deserves a visual punctuation — a rupture, a constriction, something that marks it as qualitatively different from the accumulated daily attrition. Minard's economy works against him here. The devotion to smooth encoding — consistent band form throughout — smooths over the worst moment.

Thirteen out of fifteen. The overall craft is excellent; the Berezina is a missed opportunity.

## Innovations Flagged

1. **Data anthropomorphism** — encoding human quantities through visual forms that the eye reads as organic or living, producing an experience of witnessing rather than information-receiving. This is distinct from aesthetic resonance (designed emotional effect) and from data-driven resonance (correct numbers producing sadness). It is an emergent property of the combination of: human subject matter + visual variable that reads as body/mass + continuity of the encoding across the work's duration. The rubric's Resonance dimension does not distinguish this from other forms of resonance. *Rubric anchor: Resonance. Implication: the rubric should recognize and score emergent resonance (arising from encoding choices + subject matter) as distinct from intentional-aesthetic resonance and from pure data-accuracy resonance.*

2. **Punctuation failure at rupture events** — when a continuous encoding (band, line, flow) passes through a rupture event (a battle, a catastrophe, a discontinuity), the encoding's commitment to smooth continuity may suppress the visual weight of the rupture. A separate visual grammar is needed for rupture events — something that distinguishes sudden catastrophic loss from accumulated attrition within the same continuous encoding. *Rubric anchor: Craft / Resonance. Implication: the rubric should score whether rupture events in continuous encodings receive visual punctuation appropriate to their scale.*
