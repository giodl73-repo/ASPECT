## Key Agreements

**Visual Economy and School Fidelity are both strong.** The cholera map is one-mark-per-death, one-symbol-per-pump, nothing else. All three personas give 14 or 15 on Visual Economy and 17–19 on School Fidelity. The founding grammar is clear and the execution is economical.

**Resonance is the lowest-scoring dimension.** 12–13 across all three personas — the lowest Resonance in the project except for Beck. The explanation is the same: this is a scientific/functional work. Its resonance is domain-gated (epidemiologists see the founding moment; general viewers see an interesting historical curiosity) and delayed (Snow died before recognition). Neither mode scores as high as advocacy efficacy (Nightingale's coxcomb achieved policy change) or emergent-data resonance (Minard's march moves you before you have read a number).

**Data/Story Integrity: the causal gap.**  
Minard docks 2 for causal overclaim; Nightingale docks 3; Bertin docks 2; Statistician docks 4. The spread (14–17) is moderate. All reviewers agree that spatial clustering without known mechanism is an incomplete causal argument; they disagree on how much to dock. The v1.3 rubric's Step C (causation claim) is doing useful work here — it produces a structured disagreement rather than an unstructured one. The gap between Minard (16) and the Statistician (14) is exactly the methodological disagreement: Minard accepts spatial correlation + negative evidence as near-sufficient; the Statistician insists on the missing mechanism.

**Cluster G non-confirmation.**  
No reviewer raised Visual Economy context-dependence (Cluster G). The reason is clear: the cholera map was designed for a scientific/medical audience reading a scientific/medical book. There was no competitive display environment; no accessibility-through-complexity argument; no audience who needed decoration to be reached. This non-confirmation is itself informative: Cluster G (decoration as exhibition strategy / accessibility as visual economy) applies specifically to works competing for public attention. Works for expert scientific audiences have a different Visual Economy calculation — and the current anchors serve that case well. Cluster G is a context-specific amendment, not a universal one.

## E1/E2 Split: Confirmed

This is the primary analytical purpose of work 0005. The panel has now independently confirmed what works 0003 and 0004 suggested: **E1 and E2 are genuinely distinct phenomena** that must not be combined into a single "Resonance Type V."

**E1 — Domain-gated resonance (works 0003 + 0005):**  
Present in both Beck and Snow. In both cases: the full significance of the work is available only to practitioners in the relevant domain (transit passengers who know the network; epidemiologists who know the founding). General viewers see a well-made functional diagram. The domain-specific resonance is immediate and powerful for the initiated; absent for the uninitiated. This is not about timing (contemporaneous vs. delayed) — Beck's map resonates immediately for the informed viewer; Snow's map did in 1855 and still does today. It is about audience knowledge gating.

**E2 — Delayed resonance (works 0004 + 0005):**  
Both Du Bois and Snow achieved their strongest resonance posthumously. But the *mechanism* of delay differs:
- **E2a — Epistemic delay** (Snow): The validating scientific framework (germ theory, bacterial culture) did not exist during his lifetime. The delay was a property of the scientific timeline, not of institutional resistance to his person.
- **E2b — Political-testimonial delay** (Du Bois): The framework for recognizing Black intellectual achievement existed; it was withheld by institutionalized racism. The delay was a property of political power, not of scientific readiness.

A third sub-type emerges from the Historian's review:
- **E2c — Constructed canonization** (Snow, partially): the Snow map was elevated to founding status by 20th-century science historians constructing an epidemiology origin story. The canonization was an act of later institutional curation, not natural accumulation.

**The split is confirmed. Two amendments are now available:**
- **Amend for E1**: Add Resonance Type V — Domain-Gated — for works whose resonance is audience-gated, available immediately to the initiated and unavailable to others. Works: 0003, 0005.
- **Hold E2**: The E2a/E2b/E2c distinction is too nuanced to amend confidently after two works and a multi-way split. Watch for one more confirmation before amending E2 as a single type. The Historian's third sub-type (constructed canonization) is particularly important to handle carefully.
