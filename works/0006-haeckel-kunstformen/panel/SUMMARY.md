## Key Agreements

**Craft is exceptional -- unanimous 15/15.** First work in the project to achieve perfect Craft scores from all five reviewers. The chromolithographic execution is unimpeachable; Haeckel's direct involvement in production produced work at a technical standard no reviewer could fault.

**Visual Economy is the weakest dimension -- scores cluster 8-11.** All reviewers agree non-data ink is present in significant quantity. The ornamental borders and Art Nouveau framing are the primary sources. The biological content itself is not excess (it is intrinsically dense) but its frame is. Panel consensus: borders serve attentional and compositional functions (not pure chartjunk) but are also not data. The rubric needs vocabulary for this third category.

**Scale omission is the primary data integrity failure.** Bertin, Tufte, and the Historian all flag the absence of scale information. Plates render radiolaria (0.1 mm actual) and bats (30 cm wingspan) at comparable visual sizes with no scale bar and no cross-plate comparison affordance.

---

## Key Tensions

**Does beauty equal accuracy in scientific illustration?**

Tufte and Bertin: beauty is at best a correlate of accuracy, not proof of it. The Artist: in the best scientific illustration, beauty is generated by accuracy -- rendering formally extraordinary organisms faithfully produces aesthetic effect as a consequence of fidelity. The Historian: Haeckel's documented manipulation in embryo illustrations (a separate series) complicates unconditional trust.

Surviving position: accuracy-generated beauty is real and distinguishable from imposed beauty, but requires a clean designer track record. Haeckel's mixed record explains the Artist (87) vs. Tufte (78) spread.

**Ornamental borders: chartjunk or structural scaffolding?**

Tufte: non-data, should be reduced. Artist: attentional framing, necessary to command contemplative attention. Bertin: a third category -- structural support. The disagreement surfaces a gap in Visual Economy anchors, which currently distinguish only data marks vs. chartjunk.

**E2c vs. E2d -- what kind of constructed canonization?**

The Historian proposes E2d (productive-appropriation canonization): Art Nouveau designers appropriated Haeckel's forms nearly contemporaneously, with his endorsement, producing new work in a secondary domain. Distinct from E2c (scholarly canonization, retrospective). Panel accepts as a useful distinction requiring further testing.

Innovations 55 and 58 suggest two new sub-categories: intrinsic vs. decorative complexity, and structural-attentional marks as a third category between data marks and chartjunk. Original Cluster G formulation (accessibility-via-complexity in public-display works) should remain narrow; this adds a complementary refinement.

**E2 sub-types -- Growing, hold for amendment.**
E2d (productive-appropriation canonization) proposed this work. Four candidate sub-types now active. Hold all E2 amendments until 0008-0010 reviewed.

**New Cluster H proposed -- Intrinsic Complexity (innovations 53, 55, 57).**
Three reviewers (Bertin, Tufte, Artist) across two dimensions flag: when the subject is genuinely complex, visual density is data requirement, not design failure. Watch for confirmation in 0010 (Humboldt, nine simultaneous variables). If confirmed, amend Visual Economy with intrinsic-complexity sub-criterion.
