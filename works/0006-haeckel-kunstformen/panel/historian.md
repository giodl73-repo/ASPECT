## Review

**On the founding of the scientific illustration school:**

Haeckel did not invent scientific illustration. By 1899, when the first installments of *Kunstformen* appeared, the school had a century of development behind it: Maria Sibylla Merian's botanical entomology (1705), the Florilegium tradition, Audubon's *Birds of America* (1827-1838), the great Victorian natural history surveys. What Haeckel founded was not the school but a specific synthesizing practice within it — the maximally aesthetic public-facing scientific illustration, where the argument is that nature's own forms are the most beautiful forms available to art.

This distinction matters for School Fidelity scoring. Haeckel is Type B not because scientific illustration did not exist, but because this specific mode — the public-scientific synthesis — had no clear prior exemplar at this scale and ambition. He founded a variant of an established school, not the school itself. Score accordingly: 17, not 19. The founding principles are strong and learnable; the prior tradition is too present to ignore.

**The E2c test — constructed canonization by Art Nouveau designers:**

This is the central historical question for this review. After *Kunstformen*'s publication, Art Nouveau designers — particularly in the applied arts, architecture, and decorative manufacture — began using Haeckel's biological forms directly as pattern sources. René Binet's *Esquisses décoratives* (1902) and his Paris Porte Monumentale gate (1900) directly cite radiolarian forms from Haeckel. Antoni Gaudí's organic architectural vocabulary shows strong Haeckelian resonance. Louis Sullivan's ornamental ironwork.

Is this E2c — constructed canonization — in the sense the DEGAS project has used that term? E2c requires that a community different from the intended audience elevates the work to canonical status in ways that reshape how the work is received. Here: Haeckel intended a scientific/educational audience; the designers are an artistic/craft audience who appropriated the forms. The canonization as *design source* was constructed by this secondary community.

However, I want to distinguish this from the Du Bois E2b case (political-testimonial delay) and the Snow E2c case (epistemic canonization). Haeckel's secondary canonization is not a correction of historical injustice, nor is it a retrospective scientific validation. It is an aesthetic appropriation that happened quickly (within two years of publication) and that Haeckel himself endorsed. The canonical status in design history is thus:
- Not delayed (nearly contemporaneous)
- Not politically constructed (no power differential being corrected)
- Partially authorized (Haeckel was aware and approving)

I would call this **E2d — productive-appropriation canonization**: the work's canonical status in a secondary domain (design) is constructed by that domain's community, authorized by the original maker, and productive of new work rather than merely celebratory. This sub-type should be tracked.

**On data integrity — the recapitulation controversy:**

I cannot score Data/Story Integrity without noting that Haeckel's broader scientific reputation is complicated by the recapitulation controversy. His biogenetic law ("ontogeny recapitulates phylogeny") was overstated; some of his embryo comparison illustrations, in a separate series, were demonstrably manipulated to show similarities the specimens did not possess. The *Kunstformen* plates are not directly implicated in these controversies — they show adult organisms, not developmental stages. But the broader context of Haeckel's scientific reliability matters: a scientist who falsified embryological illustrations cannot receive unconditional trust on the accuracy of morphological rendering. Dock -2 on Data/Story Integrity for this contextual reliability concern (Step A).

## Innovations Flagged

1. **E2d — productive-appropriation canonization** — a sub-type of delayed/constructed resonance in which a secondary community appropriates a work's formal vocabulary, the appropriation is authorized by the original maker, and it generates productive new work in the secondary domain. Distinct from E2a (epistemic delay), E2b (political-testimonial delay), E2c (scholarly canonization). *Rubric anchor: Resonance (Type V, E2).*

2. **Contextual reliability as Data/Story Integrity factor** — for canonical imports where the historical author has a documented record of accuracy manipulation in related work, this contextual reliability belongs in the Step A assessment even when the reviewed work itself is not directly implicated. *Rubric anchor: Data/Story Integrity (Step A).*
