## Key Agreements

**The rubric does not fail -- it fails gracefully.** All five reviewers applied the rubric to abstract work and produced scores (79-87) that feel approximately correct for a work of this quality and ambition. The scoring apparatus was not broken by the stress test. The gap is in vocabulary, not in the scoring machinery.

**Data/Story Integrity is the most contested dimension -- scores 12-15.** All reviewers dock for the color-form correspondence claims (yellow = triangle, blue = circle, red = square). These are presented as universal formal properties but are substantially culturally specific. The Step C dock (causation claim overreach) applies uniformly. The remaining disagreement (12 vs. 15) is whether the overall epistemic status of the work's claims justifies further docking beyond the specific color-form issue.

**Visual Economy and Craft are strong -- scores 13-15.** The diagrams are spare, economical pedagogical instruments. Each makes one claim and stops. No reviewer finds chartjunk in the Tuftean sense; the analog concept (compositional incoherence) is essentially absent. Craft is consistent: the Bauhaus production quality is high.

**Legibility is the lowest dimension -- scores 10-11.** All reviewers agree that the diagrams require prior theoretical orientation to be fully legible. A viewer who does not accept the framework that visual elements have formal properties will not find the color-form correspondence diagrams convincing. The tension and line-character diagrams are more immediately accessible. This is a legitimate legibility limitation for a founding work designed for initiated students.

## Key Tensions

**Kandinsky vs. Tufte on Data/Story Integrity:**

Kandinsky (15) believes his perceptual claims are substantially correct and that the compositional demonstrations validate them directly. Tufte (12) is less confident that viewer experience of a demonstration constitutes valid evidence for the claimed formal properties, and applies additional docking for the overall epistemic uncertainty.

The tension is real and illuminating: the difference between a demonstrated claim (proven by experience) and a reported claim (proven by correspondence to external data) is a genuine philosophical distinction that the rubric does not yet have vocabulary for.

**Bertin vs. Kandinsky on the visual grammar comparison:**

Bertin identifies the two grammars (retinal-operational vs. perceptual-experiential) as parallel and complementary. Kandinsky does not engage with Bertin's vocabulary -- the two systems were developed independently and are not fully commensurable. This is a genuine intellectual incompatibility that produces productive tension without a clear resolution. Neither grammar is wrong; they are at different levels of description.

**The color-form correspondence: cultural vs. formal property:**

All reviewers agree the correspondence claims are overstated. They disagree about degree. The historical context (Kandinsky notes his own synesthetic experience; later research on color-form correspondence has been mixed) is genuinely ambiguous. The scoring spread (Step C dock of 1-3 depending on reviewer) reflects legitimate disagreement about the severity of the overreach.

## The Rubric Stress Test -- Results

The brief specified that 0008 is "the rubric stress test." What did we learn?

**What the rubric handled correctly:**
- School Fidelity (Type B) correctly scored the founding grammar without penalizing for novelty
- Data/Story Integrity correctly identified and docked the color-form claim overreach
- Visual Economy correctly found the diagrams economical (no chartjunk analog present)
- Resonance (Type I) correctly recognized and scored the aesthetic-intentional resonance

**What the rubric lacked vocabulary for:**
- The demonstrative vs. reportive epistemic structure (innovation 71, 72 Kandinsky; innovation 78 Tufte)
- Compositional incoherence as the abstract analog of chartjunk (innovation 79 Tufte)
- The shared founding act vs. sole founding act distinction (innovation 82 Historian)
- The initiated-audience founding grammar vs. immediately-accessible founding grammar (innovation 83 Historian)

**Verdict:** The rubric fails gracefully. It produces approximately correct scores through dimensions that are not perfectly calibrated for abstract work but are not broken by it. The gaps are in vocabulary and sub-categorization, not in the basic scoring architecture.

## Cluster Updates

**New Cluster I proposed -- Demonstrative Encoding (innovations 71, 72, 74, 75, 77):**

Five innovations across three reviewers (Kandinsky, Bertin, Tufte, Artist) all address the same underlying phenomenon: abstract visual arguments where the diagram demonstrates its claim rather than reporting external data. The viewer verifies by experience, not by correspondence checking. This is a coherent cluster proposing an amendment to Data/Story Integrity that recognizes demonstrative encoding as a distinct epistemic structure. **Cluster I: watch for confirmation in 0009 or 0010.** (Neither is an abstract work, so confirmation may be limited to abstract-art schools; the cluster may be school-specific rather than universal.)

**Cluster H -- Intrinsic Complexity: Still watching.**
Not directly tested in 0008 (abstract diagrams are not intrinsically complex in the data-density sense). Watch in 0010 (Humboldt).

**E2 -- Still holding.**
The Historian proposes a possible E2e (diaspora-institutional canonization) for the Bauhaus diaspora. Sub-type count now at five candidates (E2a through E2e). The proliferation suggests the sub-type approach is wrong; a different taxonomy may be needed. Consider a structural taxonomy (mechanism of delay) rather than a content taxonomy (what was delayed) after 0010.

## Design Recommendations

1. **Add "demonstrative vs. reportive" distinction to Data/Story Integrity** -- for abstract works where the claim is demonstrated rather than reported, the accuracy standard is verification-by-experience rather than correspondence-to-external-data. Add as a note to the Step D anchors.

2. **Add "compositional incoherence" as the abstract analog of chartjunk** -- for Visual Economy applied to abstract works, replace the data-ink ratio criterion with: does each mark serve the compositional argument? Marks that do not are the abstract equivalent of chartjunk.

3. **Distinguish "shared founding act" in Type B scoring** -- when a school has multiple concurrent founders, Type B assessment should score the work's distinctive contribution rather than requiring the full grammar to be derivable from it alone.

4. **Reconsider E2 taxonomy** -- five candidate sub-types emerging. Consider replacing content-based sub-types (what was delayed) with mechanism-based sub-types (how it was delayed) before amending.
