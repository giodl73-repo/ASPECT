## Key Agreements

**School Fidelity is exceptional -- all reviewers give 18-19.** This is the highest average School Fidelity score in the project. The founding grammar of statistical graphics is the clearest and most durable of any school reviewed: proportional encoding, axis as scale, categorical axis, visual comparison, explicit legend. Five rules, 240 years of persistence. All reviewers agree this is a genuine, complete, derivable founding grammar.

**The Step D dock = 0 is the distinguishing feature of this work.** Playfair explicitly declared his encoding in the preface: bar length = amount; axis provides reference; the method is not more precise than a table, only more immediately comparative. He declared the data model, the encoding logic, and its limitations. This is unique in the project. The result: Data/Story Integrity scores in the 17-18 range, the highest in the project for any non-trivial work.

**Craft is the weakest dimension -- scores 11-13.** All reviewers note the limitations of hand-engraving for typographic quality. The label crowding, the slight irregularity of hand-lettered text, and the cramped trading-partner names are period-technology limitations. Tufte and Bertin introduce the distinction between period-constraint craft limitations and discretionary design choices; the panel accepts this as a useful rubric note.

## Key Tensions

**Does the founder score higher or lower than successors?**

Playfair (88.4) is the second-highest score in the project so far. Nightingale (81.2) and the Du Bois Paris charts (83.8), who worked in the tradition Playfair founded, both score lower. This seems to invert the expected order -- shouldn't a more refined later practitioner outscore the rough first inventor?

The answer is in the School Fidelity dimension: Playfair's founding grammar is so clearly stated and correctly executed that it scores 19/20, while later practitioners working in the now-established form score lower because they have more grammar to be faithful to and more ways to violate it. The first instance of a grammar, if complete and correct, scores higher than imperfect instances of a more complex later grammar.

This finding generalizes: founding works in data-type-constrained schools (few available encoding options) tend to score high because the founder can choose correctly with limited alternatives. Later practitioners face accumulated conventions and expectations.

**Baseline rule omission:**

Tufte and the Statistician both flag that Playfair did not specify the baseline rule (bars start at zero). All reviewers accept this as a genuine founding gap. The disagreement is whether to dock School Fidelity for it (Tufte withholds 1 point; others accept it as a minor gap). The consensus: it is a gap, but a small one -- Playfair's chart itself uses a zero baseline correctly, even without stating the rule.

## The "Explicit Declaration as Founding Virtue" Innovation -- Confirmed

This is the primary theoretical finding of this panel. Playfair's explicit encoding declaration (Step D dock = 0) is not just good documentation; it is a founding act. A grammar that is only practiced is a grammar that can be observed but not transmitted. A grammar that is declared is a grammar that can be taught.

Playfair's declaration did something that no other founder in this project did: he transformed his visual invention into a teachable form at the moment of its creation. Bertin did this later (1967 -- 181 years later). Neurath did it for ISOTYPE in 1936. Kandinsky did it for abstract grammar in 1926. But these were theoretical texts following decades of practice. Playfair declared in 1786, in the same document as the invention.

The rubric implication: Type B founding works should be assessed not only on whether the grammar is derivable from the work, but also on whether the grammar is declared in the work or its immediate context. Declared grammars are stronger founding acts than undeclared ones, and this distinction should influence School Fidelity scoring.

## Design Recommendations

1. **Amend School Fidelity Type B anchors** to reward explicit grammar declaration: add a criterion that distinguishes grammars that are declared (in the work or immediately adjacent documentation) from grammars that are merely derivable. Declared grammars score higher.

2. **Add baseline rule to statistical-graphics school definition** -- specify that bars must start at zero as part of the founding grammar. This is a gap in Playfair's founding that has caused persistent errors in subsequent practice.

3. **Add period-constraint note to Craft anchors** -- distinguish craft limitations imposed by production technology from discretionary design choices. Period-constrained limitations should be noted but penalized less severely than discretionary failures.

4. **Resolve E2 taxonomy** after 0010. Five candidate sub-types now active; a structural (mechanism-based) taxonomy is likely more robust.
