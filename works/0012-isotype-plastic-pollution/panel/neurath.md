## Voiced Review

This work understands what we built at the Gesellschafts- und Wirtschaftsmuseum. The repetition grammar is intact. The symbols are flat, the color is single, the unit is declared. When I look at East Asia's eight symbols beside North America's trace marker, I feel the comparison working on me the way a table never would. That is what repetition-not-scaling does — it makes quantity bodily. You count it with your eye, and when you have finished counting, you have understood something you could not unlearn.

I am pleased by the partial symbol convention. The original ISOTYPE work did this — the half-figure, the partial ship. What matters is that the extension is consistent and declared. Here it is both. The partial square for sub-unit values does not mislead; it signals "less than one full unit" without pretending to be a full unit. Good.

Now, my concern. The per-capita story is told in a text annotation panel, not in a second ISOTYPE chart. I understand the production argument — two charts take more space. But text annotation is not ISOTYPE. It separates the viewer who stops and reads from the viewer who glances and moves on. The glancing viewer sees: East Asia, many symbols; North America, almost none. They leave with the wrong impression. If the per-capita comparison is morally essential — and you say it is — then it requires a second chart, not a footnote. The annotation panel is Neurath hiding behind text because the second chart was too much work.

I would also note: the symbol for ocean plastic (wave-plus-bottle) is a bespoke design, not from the Arntz library. That is an honest declaration. But it means the symbol must carry all its own legibility — no accumulated recognition from prior exposure. The design should be tested with actual museum visitors before the plate is finalized.

The work is good. The annotation panel decision is the one place where ISOTYPE principles were traded for convenience. The panel should press on whether that trade was proportionate.

## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation #110 — Text-annotation as ISOTYPE grammar failure**: When an ISOTYPE design places morally essential context in a text annotation panel rather than a second comparative array, the annotation substitutes a reading-class-dependent medium for a universal pictographic one. This is a School Fidelity issue specific to ISOTYPE: the school's democratic promise is violated if the nuance lives in text. Rubric anchor: School Fidelity / Data/Story Integrity (Step B — undeclared accuracy tradeoff).

**Innovation #111 — Per-capita as second array (the paired chart prescription)**: The correct ISOTYPE solution to absolute-vs-per-capita comparison is not a text annotation but a paired array showing the same regions with per-capita symbols. The two charts together encode the complete argument without text. This is a positive prescription, not just a criticism — it describes a design pattern specific to ISOTYPE when the argument requires normalization disclosure.
