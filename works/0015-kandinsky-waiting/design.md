# Design — 0015 Kandinsky Waiting

## Title

*Waiting, No. 1* (2026)

The title is minimal orientation, not explanation. The composition should function before the title is read.

## Compositional Concept

The composition encodes the phenomenology of anticipatory waiting using Kandinsky's point-line-plane grammar. The formal argument:

**Waiting = a field of gathered tension held in suspension, organized around a point of maximal focus that has not yet received what approaches it.**

The composition has three formal elements:

1. **The focal point** — a dense, small circle (not quite a geometric circle — slightly imperfect, organically concentrated) placed at approximately 55% across and 48% down from the upper-left. Not centered, but nearly centered: the asymmetry creates slight unease without the dynamism of a strongly off-center position. The circle is heavy, dark blue (#1a2a5e), occupying approximately 18mm diameter at A2 scale. This is the locus of attention in waiting — where awareness collects.

2. **The approaching lines** — four lines of varying weight, each approaching the focal point from a different angle, none arriving at it. They stop short at varying distances. Line weights: 2.5pt, 2pt, 1.5pt, 0.8pt (heaviest approaches from the left, diminishing clockwise). All lines are near-horizontal to horizontal-diagonal (5°–35° range) — not the 45° dynamism of crisis but the slow, low-angle approach of accumulated time. Lines are a dark but warm gray (#3d3535), approaching the blue point but not touching it.

3. **The suspended field** — a rectangular plane occupying the full composition background, filled with a very pale warm gray (#f5f2ee). The field is active but quiet — in Kandinsky's grammar, the horizontal plane is passive; the near-white temperature is light and floating; but the sheer size of the plane relative to the point creates an environment that presses in without acting. Waiting is not nothing happening; it is everything holding.

## Formal Properties Encoded

| Phenomenological property | Formal encoding | Kandinsky grammar basis |
|--------------------------|----------------|------------------------|
| Attention gathers toward a locus | Dense dark circle; slightly off-center | Point as focal tension; dark = heavy = gravitational |
| Suspension — action not yet complete | Lines stop short of the point | Line with truncated terminus; incompletion as formal element |
| Multiple simultaneous vectors of attention | Four lines from different angles | Multiple approaching lines; field of convergent directions |
| Time feels thick and accumulated | Slow approach angles (5°–35°) | Low angle = slow energy; near-horizontal = cold/passive direction |
| Ambient field is charged but not urgent | Large, very pale warm-gray plane | Horizontal plane = passive; light color = floating; size = environmental weight |
| One vector is dominant | Heaviest line from left | Line weight as urgency; left-origin as temporal precedence |
| The point does not receive what approaches | Gap between line termini and point | Formal incompletion; unresolved tension |

## Color System

| Color | Hex | Formal role | Kandinsky basis |
|-------|-----|-------------|-----------------|
| Deep blue | #1a2a5e | Focal point | Blue = deep, receding, concentration; heavy = gravitational |
| Dark warm gray | #3d3535 | Approaching lines | Neutral urgency; not red (crisis) not blue (passivity) — gray is suspended |
| Pale warm gray | #f5f2ee | Background plane | Near-white = light/floating; warm (not cool) = inhabited, not empty |

Color restriction is intentional: waiting is not colorful. The suppression of color is itself a formal argument about the chromatic character of the waiting state.

## Composition Geometry

Placement of focal point: 55% horizontal, 48% vertical (center-right, center-slightly-above-middle).

Line origination points:
- Line 1 (heaviest, 2.5pt): from left edge at 55% height; terminates 35mm from focal point
- Line 2 (2pt): from upper-left at 30%×20%; terminates 50mm from focal point
- Line 3 (1.5pt): from lower-left at 15%×75%; terminates 65mm from focal point
- Line 4 (0.8pt): from right edge at 60% height; terminates 80mm from focal point — faintest line from opposite direction

The lines vary in their proximity to the focal point inversely with their weight: the heaviest line is closest; the lightest is farthest. This encodes the unevenness of waiting — some threads of attention are almost upon the event; others are still far off.

## What is Not In This Composition

No clock. No door. No figure. No diagonal (too dynamic for waiting). No red (too urgent/aggressive). No yellow (too advancing/sharp). No curved lines (too yielding). No geometric triangles (too aggressive). No text in the image itself.

The absence of representational elements is the school's requirement. The absence of warm colors and dynamic angles is the composition's specific formal argument.

## Rendering Notes

- Format: A2 (420×594mm), portrait orientation, for gallery display
- Background: unpainted or very lightly washed warm gray on heavy watercolor paper — not a digital flat fill; the texture of the support is part of the composition's formal character
- Focal point: painted (gouache or oil, high pigment concentration), not digital — the slight imperfection of a painted circle is compositionally required (a perfect digital circle would introduce the wrong formal property — precision rather than concentration)
- Lines: drawn with a ruling pen or fine brush; slight variation in line quality is acceptable — waiting is not mechanical
- Scale: the 18mm focal point at A2 scale is large enough to read as dominant from 1.5m; small enough that the field dwarfs it
