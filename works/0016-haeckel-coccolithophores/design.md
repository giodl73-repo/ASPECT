# Design — 0016 Haeckel Coccolithophores

## Visual Structure

A comparative scientific illustration plate: five species, each displayed in a vertical column. Each column contains: (1) top-down view of individual coccolith showing full plate geometry; (2) oblique view showing three-dimensional plate structure; (3) partial coccosphere showing how plates interlock on the cell surface. A scale bar is provided per species (schematic equalization: all displayed at comparable visual size; individual scale bars declare actual size). The background is deep navy (#0c1525) — SEM-convention dark background — serving both scientific context-familiarity and the aesthetic priority of making the white/cream calcium carbonate plates visually vivid.

## Layout Specification

Portrait A1 (594x841mm) — the scale needed to show diagnostic morphological features.

Title: Five Coccolithophore Species: Structural Morphology and Diagnostic Features

Subtitle: Each species illustrated at schematic equalization; individual scale bars show actual coccolith diameter.

Five column headers (species names in italic), then three rows: (1) top-down views; (2) oblique views; (3) partial coccospheres. Diagnostic feature labels with leader lines. Scale bars per species below oblique view.

| Visual Variable | What it encodes | Justification |
|----------------|-----------------|---------------|

| Scale bar | Actual size declaration | Required scientific information |
| Leader lines | Connecting label to specific structural feature | Standard scientific illustration convention |

## The Five Species: Diagnostic Features Illustrated

**Col. 1 — Emiliania huxleyi** (3.5 micron coccolith, scale bar: 1 micron = 3mm displayed)
Top-down: murolith plate showing slotted distal shield; 30-35 radiating elements visible; central tube opening. Oblique: shows tube cycle and shield cycle in three-dimensional relationship. Coccosphere: densely packed muroliths. Diagnostic accent (pale gold): the slotted shield perforations — unique vs. Gephyrocapsa.

**Col. 2 — Coccolithus pelagicus** (13 micron coccolith, scale bar: 5 microns = 3mm displayed)
Top-down: large placolith; prominent central opening; proximal and distal shields interlocking; robust rim elements. Oblique: shows shield height and interlocking bridge structure; significantly more massive than other species. Diagnostic accent (pale gold): the bridge structure spanning the central opening.

**Col. 3 — Calcidiscus leptoporus** (8 micron coccolith, scale bar: 3 microns = 3mm displayed)
Top-down: smooth solid placolith; no central process; near-perfect radial symmetry; no perforation. Oblique: low-profile plate; smooth surface; minimal structural elaboration. Diagnostic accent: none required — the absence of features IS the diagnostic.

**Col. 4 — Gephyrocapsa oceanica** (5 micron coccolith, scale bar: 2 microns = 3mm displayed)
Top-down: murolith similar to E. huxleyi; but prominent bridge element crossing central opening. Oblique: shows bridge element in relief. Diagnostic accent (pale gold): the bridge element — the single feature distinguishing from E. huxleyi.

**Col. 5 — Umbilicosphaera sibogae** (7 micron coccolith, scale bar: 3 microns = 3mm displayed)
Top-down: open central area (umbilicus); perforated shield segments; radial symmetry with larger interelement spaces. Oblique: open, lattice-like structure; most visually distinctive of the five. Diagnostic accent (pale gold): the umbilical opening and perforated shield margins.

## Intrinsic Complexity Assessment (H Amendment)

Under rubric v1.5 amendment H: biological morphological complexity is intrinsic. The diagnostic features of these species (slotted shield perforations; bridge element; umbilical opening) require sufficient mark density to be visible and identifiable. Total mark count is high; it is entirely intrinsic. Additive complexity in this design: zero. No decorative borders, ornamental framing, or illustrative backgrounds.

## Rendering Notes

- Medium: gouache and fine-detail pen on black illustration board, or digital equivalent with texture applied
- The cream-to-white value range for calcium carbonate structure must show three-dimensionality convincingly; requires careful value control; cannot be achieved with flat fills
- Scale bars: physically accurate measurement marks; each labeled with unit and magnification factor
- Leader lines: horizontal arm at label followed by diagonal approach to feature; lines should not cross
- Pale gold accent: maximum two structural features per species — overuse eliminates diagnostic function
