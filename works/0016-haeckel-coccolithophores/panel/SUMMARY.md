## Key Tensions

**Historian's convention-layer finding:** The scientific illustration school spans two periods (Haeckel white-background tradition; modern SEM dark-background tradition). Innovation #138 proposes that School Fidelity scoring should specify which convention layer the work claims.

**Artist vs. everyone else on differential visual allocation:** The Artist recommends Umbilicosphaera sibogae receive more visual space. All other reviewers affirm schematic equalization as the correct scientific convention. Genuine tension between school fidelity and visual effect; the panel does not resolve it.

**Primary vs. secondary audience legibility:** The Audience lens flags that for the general-public museum context, the plate is domain-gated without a substantial caption zone. Innovation #139 names the gap: caption zone content is as important as the illustration for the general-public audience.

## Design Revision Recommendations

1. **Write the caption zone content** — each species: distinguishing features, ecological role, climate significance (CO2 absorption). Add a general coccolithophore introduction for the museum application.
2. **Consider differential visual allocation for Umbilicosphaera sibogae** — test a 15% wider column; if implemented, declare the exception to schematic equalization.
3. **Declare the SEM convention choice explicitly** — add a sentence in the subtitle or caption.
4. **Add a size-comparison inset** — all five species at true relative scale, no schematic equalization; place at plate edge.
5. **Validate the pale gold accent selection** — confirm the accented feature is the single most distinguishing feature vs. nearest relative for each species.
