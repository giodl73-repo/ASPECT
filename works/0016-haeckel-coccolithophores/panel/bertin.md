## Voiced Review

Let me begin with the encoding grammar. The primary visual variable is shape — rendered form encoding species identity. This is correct for scientific illustration: shape is the only variable that can encode morphological structure at the specificity required for taxonomic identification. Size is secondary (the scale bars declare it); color is tertiary and restricted to material identity (calcium carbonate) and diagnostic accent. Position is organizational (the column-and-row structure). The grammar is clear and correctly matched to the data type.

The pale gold diagnostic accent is a variable addition that is not in the historical scientific illustration grammar — it is an extension. But it is declared and used consistently at maximum two features per species. Its function is selective: the viewer's eye is drawn to the accent without instruction, which is exactly the retinal efficiency criterion for the selective visual property. A cautious reviewer might object that diagnostic features should be identifiable without accent. I counter: in a dark-background five-column plate, selective emphasis on the single distinguishing feature accelerates identification without falsifying anything. The accent does not change the shape; it directs attention to a shape that is already there.

The intrinsic complexity finding is clear. The murolith structure of Emiliania huxleyi — 30-35 radiating elements, a tube cycle, a shield cycle — cannot be reduced to fewer marks without losing the diagnostic information. The perforations in the distal shield, which distinguish it from Gephyrocapsa oceanica, are the information. The mark density required to show them IS the scientific content. Under rubric H, I score Visual Economy on excess above intrinsic necessity. Excess above intrinsic necessity here: zero.

The population-not-individual limitation is honest and declared. True population variation would require 10-20 specimens per species. For a five-species comparative plate, three views (top, oblique, coccosphere) is the appropriate compromise between completeness and legibility. I accept the partial score.

## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation #134 — Selective accent on diagnostic features as retinal efficiency tool**: In multi-species scientific illustration plates, a selective color accent (distinct from the primary morphological rendering) applied to the one or two features that distinguish each species from its nearest relative accelerates identification without adding non-data marks. The accent is retinal-efficiency for taxonomic comparison: it exploits the selective property of color hue to direct the eye to the distinguishing feature before the viewer has consciously searched for it. Rubric anchor: Visual Economy / Legibility.

This is the H amendment's clearest possible case. Works 0006 (Haeckel) and 0016 together establish the morphological-illustration H application as a pattern. Rubric anchor: Visual Economy (H amendment confirmation).
