## Voiced Review

Haeckel's plates in Kunstformen der Natur used white backgrounds — the 19th-century natural history illustration convention, which treated the organism against neutral space. The SEM (scanning electron microscope) dark-background convention emerged in the mid-20th century when electron micrographs became the primary medium for coccolithophore documentation. The two conventions coexist in the literature; this work chooses the SEM convention for the scientific audience and declares the choice in the context note.

This is a defensible historical position. The scientific illustration school includes both the Haeckel tradition (white background, color-rendered morphology) and the modern SEM tradition (dark background, high-contrast structural rendering). A work operating in the Haeckel tradition would choose white background, color-rendered calcium carbonate, and the decorative compositional arrangement that Haeckel favored. This work operates in the modern scientific tradition, which is the correct choice for a scientific journal plate audience. The school fidelity score reflects that both traditions are legitimate within the school.

The five-species selection is historically well-chosen. Emiliania huxleyi is the most abundant coccolithophore on Earth and the most studied. Coccolithus pelagicus is among the oldest extant species, with a fossil record extending 60 million years. Calcidiscus leptoporus and Gephyrocapsa oceanica are both biogeographically important and regularly confused in the literature — showing them side by side on the same plate addresses a real taxonomic need. Umbilicosphaera sibogae is less studied but morphologically distinctive. The selection represents the diversity of the group well.

The schematic equalization convention — displaying all species at comparable visual size with individual scale bars — is the established convention for taxonomic comparison plates since at least the 1970s. Young et al. (2003) uses exactly this approach. The design is historically and technically correct.

## Innovations Flagged

**Innovation #138 — Convention-layer declaration in multi-tradition schools**: When a school has accumulated multiple historical conventions (Haeckel white-background tradition; modern SEM dark-background tradition), a work operating in one convention should declare which layer it is working in, rather than simply claiming school membership. The declaration resolves apparent anachronism (SEM convention in a school founded before electron microscopy) and allows the reviewer to score fidelity to the correct tradition.
