## Key Agreements

**The contemplative-study context genuinely lifts legibility.** All five reviewers acknowledge this. Compared to the public-display context of work 0007, the book reader extracts substantially more information — denser arrays are readable, finer distinctions are available, the text supplements what the chart cannot carry alone. The legibility scores (13-14/15) are higher than they would be for the same grammar in a museum-display context.

**School fidelity is strong** across all reviewers (17-18/20). The grammar is faithfully applied; the adaptation to book scale and two-color printing is within the school's principles. The grammar's portability is confirmed.

**Craft is the strongest dimension** by consensus (14/15 unanimously). The Knopf production is professional and consistent.

---

## Key Tensions

**Tufte vs. Neurath/Nightingale/Audience on Visual Economy:**

Tufte scores 11/15; others score 12-13/15. The tension is the Cluster G boundary condition. Tufte argues that the book context reduces the cognitive-economy allowance — the educated 1939 book reader is more bar-chart-literate than the Vienna working-class museum visitor, so the ISOTYPE repetition is less efficient relative to available alternatives. Neurath and Nightingale argue that the 1939 general readership still benefits from pictographic encoding over abstract charts, and that the decorative elements contribute to the book's visual argument.

Tufte's Cluster G boundary condition innovation (both low-literacy AND public-display required for full Cluster G allowance) is the precise statement of this tension. The panel does not resolve it — the question of where the boundary falls is genuinely contested.

**Chart self-sufficiency vs. text-chart argument unit:**

Neurath acknowledges that the book charts are not self-sufficient — they function as evidence for the prose argument, not as complete visual arguments. Nightingale and the Audience lens treat this as appropriate to the book format. Tufte treats it as a mild integrity concern (if the chart depends on the text, the chart alone is misleading). The panel's working consensus: the text-image unit is the correct scoring unit for a book, and assessed as such, the legibility is good.

---

## Cluster G Boundary Condition — Confirmed

Work 0019 establishes the Cluster G boundary condition: cognitive economy (the Cluster G allowance) applies when BOTH the audience is low-literacy AND the context is public-display (time-constrained). Either condition alone is insufficient for the full allowance.

In 0019:
- Audience: general educated reader (not low-literacy) — allowance shrinks
- Context: contemplative study (not time-constrained) — allowance shrinks further

Result: standard data-ink efficiency is closer to the right criterion in this context than full cognitive economy. The ISOTYPE grammar's repetition-not-scaling choice is somewhat less efficient here than in the museum context, and the decorative elements carry less justification.

This is not a finding about 0019 alone — it is a rubric clarification that applies to all future works where Cluster G is invoked.
