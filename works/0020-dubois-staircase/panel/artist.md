# Panel Review — Artist on 0020: Du Bois Staircase-Spiral

## Opening

I am asked: is this work memorable? Will someone who sees it once remember it?

Yes. Of the sixty charts in the Du Bois Paris suite, this is the one that contemporary designers reproduce most often. Not the bar charts — those are accurate but conventional. Not the straight-line graphs — those are clear but forgettable. The staircase-spiral. The one that doesn't look like anything else.

That memorability is not accidental. Du Bois invented a form. Invented forms are inherently memorable precisely because they have no competition: you cannot confuse this chart with another chart because no other chart looks like this. The visual schema is unique.

## On Resonance: Maximum Score

I am giving this work 15/15 on Resonance. Let me explain why.

The staircase-spiral achieves the highest form of Type VI Delayed/Politically-constructed resonance. The chart was:
1. Created in 1900 under conditions of systematic political suppression
2. Not reviewed or discussed in mainstream scholarship for 118 years
3. Rediscovered in 2018 and immediately recognized as among the finest works of data visualization ever produced
4. Now reproduced and discussed more widely than the Bar charts of Playfair, the coxcomb of Nightingale, and the march map of Minard

The speed of the 2018 recognition — immediate, unanimous, widespread — is itself evidence of the form's power. A work that required 118 years of suppression to suppress, and that achieved canonical status within months of recovery, has extraordinary resonance. The resonance was not delayed by the quality of the work; it was delayed by politics. Politics cannot reduce the quality; it can only delay the recognition.

Additionally: the staircase-spiral achieves Type I (Aesthetic-Intentional) resonance on its own terms. The form is beautiful. The expanding staircase, hand-drawn in red and black, growing outward from the center — this is an object of visual pleasure that exists independently of what it shows.

**Score: 15/15**

## Innovation Flagged

**Innovation: Suppression-resistant resonance** — Rubric anchor: Resonance (Type VI). The Du Bois staircase-spiral's Type VI resonance has an unusual property: the 118-year suppression strengthens rather than weakens the evidence for the work's power. If a work requires active suppression to delay its recognition, the act of suppression is itself evidence of the work's threat — its resonance potential was recognized by those who suppressed it. Work that achieves immediate recognition upon recovery, after a politically-constructed delay, exhibits suppression-resistant resonance: the delay demonstrates the power, the recovery confirms it. This is the strongest form of Type VI Delayed/Politically-constructed resonance in the project.
