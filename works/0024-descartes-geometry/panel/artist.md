# Artist Review — La Géométrie Diagrams (1637)

## Opening

The experience of Euclidean proof diagrams, as the Euclid panel noted, carries the aesthetic of mathematical necessity — the feeling that the conclusion could not have been otherwise. Descartes's diagrams carry something similar, but with a different emotional character. Where Euclid's necessity is geological (it was always true, waiting to be discovered), Descartes's necessity is more like a bridge being built in real time. You watch the algebraic and geometric modes converge on the same value, and the resonance is: these two different languages are saying the same thing.

## The Resonance of Convergence

The unit construction diagrams have this character most strongly. The diagram shows a geometric construction; the text shows an algebraic equation; the viewer sees that the construction produces the same result as the equation. The moment of seeing this is not the same as the Euclidean moment. Euclid's proofs say: this IS so, and here is why. Descartes's proofs say: these two things ARE the same, and here is the bridge.

The aesthetic category is correspondence rather than necessity. The resonance depends on the viewer holding both representations in mind simultaneously and perceiving their equivalence. This is a cognitively demanding form of resonance — it requires more of the viewer than Euclid's geometric necessity — but when it fires, it is distinctive and powerful.

For a viewer without the prerequisite knowledge (Euclidean geometry + algebraic notation), the resonance is entirely unavailable. This is the strongest domain-gating in the project: you need two disciplines to access the full resonance, not one.

## Scoring

**Resonance: 12/15**

Type V (Domain-Gated) with dual-discipline gating. The resonance is strong within the domain; the domain requirement is unusually high (two prerequisite disciplines). Score reflects the resonance quality for domain-initiated viewers; the dual-discipline gate is noted but does not reduce the primary score.

**Other dimensions:** I score Legibility, Visual Economy, Resonance, and Craft only.

**Legibility: 12/15**

For the primary audience: high. For contemporary general audiences: domain-gated completely.

**Visual Economy: 14/15**

Marks are logically necessary throughout. Exceptional economy by the demonstrative standard.

**Craft: 13/15**

Clean period execution. The figures have the quality of well-made scientific illustration — precise, consistent, without flourish.

## Summary

Artist scores: Resonance **12/15**, Legibility **12/15**, Visual Economy **14/15**, Craft **13/15**

The resonance of algebraic-geometric correspondence (two languages saying the same thing) is a distinct aesthetic mode from Euclidean mathematical necessity. Both are genuine, both are powerful, both are completely domain-gated. The school produces two distinct resonance types across two historical periods.
