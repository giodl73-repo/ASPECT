# Bertin Review — VDQI Diagrams (1983)

## Opening

I published *Sémiologie Graphique* sixteen years before Tufte published *The Visual Display of Quantitative Information*. We approach the same subject from different traditions: I from French cartographic semiotics; Tufte from American political science and statistical theory. Our prescriptions are often the same. Our theoretical frameworks are different.

I review Tufte's meta-visualization diagrams as I review any diagrams: by auditing the visual variables in use and asking whether each variable is matched to the information it encodes.

## Visual Variable Audit of Meta-Visualization Diagrams

The meta-visualization diagrams have a distinctive structure: they encode information ABOUT other visualizations. Let me identify the visual variables specific to this class.

**Primary variables (inherited from the analyzed visualization):**
The diagrams include copies or representations of other charts. The visual variables in those charts (position, size, hue, etc.) are present in the meta-visualization for the purpose of critique, not for the purpose of encoding new data. A reviewer must distinguish between variables that encode the analyzed work's data (quoted encoding) and variables that encode Tufte's critical argument (meta-encoding).

**Meta-encoding variables (Tufte's critical apparatus):**
- **Annotation arrows:** Direction points to the element being critiqued; position indicates which mark is problematic
- **Callout text:** Encodes Tufte's critical judgment about the indicated mark
- **Annotation boxes:** Group multiple marks being addressed together
- **Before/after sequence:** Position (left/right or before/after in the text) encodes the before/after temporal relationship
- **Removal highlighting:** In the data-ink removal sequences, marks that are removed between versions carry the encoding "this is chartjunk"

These meta-encoding variables are internally consistent and legible. The annotation apparatus works: the viewer sees the problematic mark, reads the annotation, understands the critique.

Innovation #199: **Meta-encoding visual variables** — meta-visualization works that analyze other visualizations require a distinct layer of visual variables to encode the critic's argument about the analyzed work. These meta-encoding variables (annotation arrows, callout labels, removal sequences, before/after position) constitute a grammar specific to meta-visualization. Tufte deploys them consistently; the consistency constitutes a learnable meta-visualization grammar.

## Comparison to Bertin's Own Framework

I note that Tufte's framework and my own overlap substantially on the core prescriptions (use position for quantitative data; avoid unordered variables for ordered data; minimize non-data marks) but differ in structure. My framework is explicitly a grammar — a systematic description of visual variable properties. Tufte's framework is principled but not formally grammatical — it is a set of prescriptions derived from good examples and bad examples, not from a theoretical account of how visual variables work.

This is not a criticism; it is a different rhetorical strategy. My framework aims for theoretical completeness; Tufte's aims for practical persuasion. His approach is more accessible; mine is more generalizable. Both are needed.

## Scoring

**School Fidelity (Type B): 16/20**

The information-architecture school is established with learnable principles and a consistent meta-encoding grammar. I dock slightly for: (1) the grammar is principled but not formally systematic in the way a grammar should be — the principles are not derived from a theory; they are derived from good examples; (2) the scope question is unresolved.

**Data/Story Integrity: 15/20**

Demonstrative elements valid. Lie factor calculations have undeclared measurement choices (Step A). Score: 15.

**Legibility: 14/15**

Highly legible for the primary professional audience. The meta-encoding grammar is consistent and parseable. The annotation apparatus is clear.

**Visual Economy: 14/15**

The book practices its own economy principle. The meta-encoding apparatus (annotation arrows, callouts) is minimal and functional — structural-attentional marks that justify themselves by directing attention. Score: 14.

**Resonance: 13/15**

Type III (Advocacy-Efficacy). Strong; the book reformed practice. Type VI (no delay — immediate recognition).

**Craft: 15/15**

Exceptional. The typography is the exemplar of the book's argument.

## Summary

Bertin total: **87/100**

The meta-encoding visual variables (Innovation #199) are the most important finding from this review. Tufte's annotation apparatus constitutes a learnable meta-visualization grammar that is the basis for the information-architecture school he founds. My own framework provides the theoretical foundation; Tufte's work provides the practical demonstration apparatus. They are complementary.
