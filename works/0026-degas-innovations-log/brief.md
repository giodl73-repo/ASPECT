# Brief — 0026 The DEGAS Innovations Log as Visual Texture

## The Dataset

The DEGAS project has logged 202 innovations across 25 works, distributed across 6 rubric dimensions and 16 adopted clusters. This work uses that log as raw material — not to chart it for analysis, but to make it visible as an aesthetic object.

## The Premise

Innovation in a rubric system does not distribute evenly. Some works generate dense clusters of new insights; others confirm existing patterns with minimal disruption. The 202 innovations, plotted as marks across 25 works and 6 dimensions, form an irregular but patterned field. The goal is not a legible chart of who innovated what — it is a texture you experience as accumulating density and color, where the eye senses richness before the mind parses categories.

## The Argument (Aesthetic, Not Informational)

Data art does not make an argument you read; it makes a pattern you feel. This work asks: what does 202 innovations feel like as a visual field? The answer is: uneven, clustered, warmly colored in the middle (adopted clusters), cooler at the edges (forming or unconfirmed innovations), and surprisingly dense in the region of works 0001 and 0006-0010 — the canonical imports that seeded the rubric's first waves of development.

## Audience and Context

Gallery or screen display. The intended viewer is someone familiar with the DEGAS project who will experience the texture as a kind of portrait of the system they know — recognition before comprehension. The work also functions for unfamiliar viewers as pure pattern, which is the data art school's primary claim.
