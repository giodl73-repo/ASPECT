# Design Specification — 0026 The DEGAS Innovations Log as Visual Texture

## Canvas

Portrait orientation, 60cm × 90cm (or screen equivalent at 1200 × 1800px). White ground. No gridlines. No title block inside the image — the title is mounted separately as a wall label.

## Encoding System

### Axes

- **Horizontal axis**: Work number, 0001 (far left) to 0025 (far right). 25 columns, evenly spaced. Column labels appear as small numerals (7pt, light gray) along the bottom edge only. No axis line.
- **Vertical axis**: Innovation number within each work (1st innovation from that work at top, last at bottom). The vertical extent of each column is proportional to the number of innovations that work produced. Works with 1–2 innovations are short columns; works with 8–12 innovations (0001, 0006, 0009, 0010, 0011) are tall columns. The columns do not align at a common baseline — they hang from a common top edge. The effect: a cityscape of uneven towers.

### Mark Type (6 types by rubric dimension)

Each innovation is a single mark. The mark type encodes which rubric dimension was touched:

| Dimension | Mark Type |
|-----------|-----------|
| School Fidelity | Circle (●) — 6pt |
| Data/Story Integrity | Diamond (◆) — 6pt |
| Legibility | Horizontal dash (—) — 8pt × 2pt |
| Visual Economy | Vertical bar (|) — 2pt × 8pt |
| Resonance | Asterisk (*) — 6pt, 6-pointed |
| Craft | Triangle (▲) — 5pt |

Mark types are drawn in a unified, minimal style — all at similar visual weight, no outlines, filled flat. The viewer who studies the legend can decode dimension; the viewer who does not will simply experience the texture.

### Color (cluster membership)

Each innovation is colored by its cluster assignment:

**Warm (adopted, rubric-amending clusters — orange-red family):**
- Clusters A, B, C, D: burnt sienna (#C84B31)
- Clusters E1, E2, F, G: amber (#D4882B)
- Clusters H, I, J: ochre (#C9A227)

**Cool (forming or unconfirmed clusters — blue-teal family):**
- Innovations in forming clusters or watch status: slate blue (#4A6FA5)
- Innovations in HOLD status: cool gray-blue (#7B9EB0)

**Neutral (unclassified innovations, no cluster assigned):**
- Light warm gray (#B8A99A)

The color palette is muted and slightly desaturated — not primary colors. The effect from a distance is a warm central mass (the rubric-amending clusters in the early-middle works) with cooler tones at the periphery (newer, less-settled innovations).

### Mark Density

Marks within each column are arranged in a close grid: 3 marks per row (if more than 3 innovations), rows stacked from top to bottom. The horizontal spacing between marks within a column is 5pt; vertical spacing is 5pt. The column width is 16pt (enough for 3 marks with spacing). Dense columns (8–12 innovations) are visually heavier; sparse columns (1–2) are light. This density difference is immediately perceptible at reading distance.

### Spacing Between Columns

Columns are separated by 8pt gaps. The overall grid reads as a continuous field with columns emerging from close inspection.

## Typographic Elements

- **Bottom axis labels**: work numbers 01–25, 7pt, Futura Light or equivalent geometric sans, light gray (#AAAAAA)
- **Legend**: mounted as a separate panel to the right of the image, 15cm × 15cm. Lists mark types (by dimension) and color key (by cluster family). 8pt, same typeface. The legend is deliberately separate — the image is clean; the key is available for those who want it.
- **No title inside the image.** Wall label reads: "The DEGAS Innovations Log as Visual Texture / 202 marks / 25 works / 6 rubric dimensions / 16 clusters / 2026"

## Compositional Effect

The expected visual result:

1. **Dense left-center region**: Works 0001–0010 produced the majority of rubric-amending innovations. This region is warm-colored and dense — the hotspot of the field.
2. **Mid-field cooling**: Works 0011–0020 produced fewer rubric amendments; more of the innovations here are in forming/unconfirmed clusters. The color cools and the mark density decreases.
3. **Sparse right edge**: Works 0021–0025 produced Cluster O (dynamic visualization grammar) innovations — notable but relatively few marks. These appear as a small cool-blue cluster at the far right.
4. **Overall gestalt**: an irregular field that is neither random nor gridded — the pattern of intellectual activity in a self-improving system. The rubric's evolution is visible as accumulating color density in certain regions.

## School Grammar Compliance

This work operates within the data art school's constitutive principles:
- Data as material: the innovation log is the dataset; marks are individual innovations
- Aesthetic primacy: the visual field is the experience; precise readback is not the goal
- Legibility is negotiable: the viewer cannot extract exact innovation numbers from the texture; the overall pattern communicates what individual readback cannot
- Form follows feeling: the irregular-column hanging structure was chosen for its resemblance to natural formations (stalactites, rainfall) — an analog for the uneven, organic accumulation of insight

## What the Work Does Not Do

This is not a dashboard. The innovation count per work is not labeled numerically. The specific cluster assignments are not readable from the marks alone without the legend. The work does not argue that one school or dimension is "better" — it makes the pattern of activity visible as a texture, and leaves interpretation to the viewer.
