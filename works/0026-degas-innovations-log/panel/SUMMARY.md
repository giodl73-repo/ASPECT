## Key Agreements

**The internal column grid is the most correctable flaw.** Kandinsky, Artist, and Bertin all flag it. The 3-per-row arrangement creates mechanical regularity that fights the organic hanging-column structure. Bertin names it precisely: a phantom variable — visual regularity that the eye decodes as an encoding but encodes nothing.

**Column height is the strongest encoding.** The skyline of innovation density is immediately readable. All reviewers note this as the work's most successful element.

**The separate legend is the right structural decision.** All reviewers accept that the image field should be annotation-free. Execution risk: gallery visitors may miss the separate legend entirely.

## Key Tensions

**Tufte vs. Kandinsky/Artist on Visual Economy:** Tufte reaches 9/15 applying the school's own criterion; Kandinsky and Artist reach 13-14. This tension exposes a rubric gap: data art's aesthetic-primary economy criterion is not cleanly addressed by Cluster H (intrinsic complexity). Two reviewers flag this as innovation #203 and #205 — candidate for Cluster Q.

**Insider vs. outsider Resonance:** Kandinsky/Artist score 13/15 (DEGAS-aware recognition); Audience scores 10/15 (aesthetic texture only). The work's resonance is context-gated.
