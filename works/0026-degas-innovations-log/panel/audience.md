# Audience Lens — 0026 The DEGAS Innovations Log as Visual Texture

## School Fidelity /20 → 14

I'm the gallery visitor who has never heard of DEGAS. I see: a field of small colored marks arranged in columns of varying height, warm-colored on the left, cooler on the right. I don't know what this represents. There is no title on the image. The wall label is small. I am willing to read the wall label.

After reading: "202 marks / 25 works / 6 rubric dimensions / 16 clusters." I now know this represents something specific, but I still don't know what "innovations" or "clusters" or "rubric dimensions" mean. This work is designed for an insider audience. For that audience, it probably scores higher. For me, it is attractive but opaque.

The school fidelity question from my angle: does this feel like data art? Yes — it feels like a work where data has been turned into something to look at, not read. That claim is successfully made.

## Data/Story Integrity /20 → 15

I cannot verify the data. I trust that 202 marks represent 202 innovations. The warm/cool color pattern seems meaningful — more warmth on the left, more coolness on the right — and I accept it as representing something real about where innovation concentrated.

## Legibility /15 → 8

The separate legend panel is the right idea but the execution asks a lot of me. I have to find the legend, read it, hold its contents in working memory, then look back at the image and try to decode individual marks at 6pt. At gallery viewing distance, I cannot distinguish a circle from a diamond from an asterisk. The mark type encoding is entirely unavailable to me without significant effort and probably a magnifying glass.

What I can read: column heights (some works generated more), the warm/cool color distribution, the overall feeling of the field. That is a real reading. It is incomplete, but it is something.

## Visual Economy /15 → 12

The work does not feel cluttered. The white space is generous. The marks are small and do not fight each other. The muted palette is easy on the eye. Whatever the work is trying to do, it does not try to do it loudly.

## Resonance /15 → 10

For me, as an outsider, the resonance is primarily aesthetic: an interesting patterned field. The emotional register is quiet and intellectual. I sense that this represents something significant without being able to say what. That is a partial resonance — Type I fires at low intensity because I lack the context to make it fire fully.

For an insider, I imagine the resonance is significantly higher — recognition of a system they know, made visible as a portrait. That version of the work probably scores 13–14 on Resonance.

## Craft /15 → 12

The work looks deliberately made. The palette is controlled. The mark sizes are consistent. I cannot evaluate the fine detail without getting much closer than the gallery setup encourages.

## My Total: 71/100

I appreciate what this work is trying to do. But for an audience member who is not a DEGAS insider, the work withholds most of its meaning. This is a legitimate data art choice — the school accepts selective legibility — but I want to name what the general viewer loses. The work is more beautiful than informative to me, and in a gallery that is acceptable. In an educational context, it would need significant supplementary material.
