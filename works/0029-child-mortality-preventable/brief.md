# Brief — 0029 Child Mortality by Preventable Cause (Global 2023)

## The Argument

Five million children under five die annually from preventable causes. The leading causes — pneumonia, diarrhea, malaria, neonatal sepsis, malnutrition — are all treatable for under $20 per case at 90% coverage rates. The gap between current mortality and 90% coverage mortality is not a resource gap; it is a political choice. This work makes that gap the visual argument.

## Design Approach

Split bar chart (butterfly/diverging format). Left bars: current annual deaths by cause. Right bars: projected deaths at 90% treatment coverage. The gap between left and right bars IS the argument — the visual space between the two bars represents preventable deaths if coverage were achieved.

Second-iteration design priorities:
- Gestalt argument immediately available (Type IV resonance) — the gap is visible before any label is read
- Precision-as-defiance (Du Bois principle) — exact numbers shown; no rounding to impressionism
- Honest unflattering data — the current death rates are not minimized or hedged
- Advocacy efficacy for health policymakers (Type III) — specific, actionable conclusion

## Score Target

85-90. The gap-as-argument structure is designed to be immediately accessible; the precision of exact numbers satisfies Du Bois. The main challenge is the trend dimension (Rosling will ask: are these deaths improving or worsening year-over-year?).
