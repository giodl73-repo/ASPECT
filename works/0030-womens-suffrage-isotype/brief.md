# Brief — 0030 History of Women's Suffrage

## The Story

New Zealand granted women's suffrage in 1893 — the first country. Saudi Arabia extended voting rights to women in 2015. In between: 122 years, approximately 50 countries, a cascade of adoption with clusters by decade and by region. The visual argument: suffrage is not a single event but a wave — and the wave's shape reveals political patterns (post-WWI cluster, post-WWII decolonization cluster, post-Cold War cluster).

## Form Innovation

Standard ISOTYPE grammar encodes quantity through repetition — a row of worker symbols = N workers, where N is counted. This work innovates on ISOTYPE grammar: it encodes both count (number of countries with suffrage) AND time (when each country achieved it) by using position rather than stack-count as the temporal variable. Each country is one flag-pictogram. Chronological position within a grid (read left-to-right, top-to-bottom) encodes the order of adoption. Color of each pictogram encodes geographic region.

## Innovation Target

Temporal ISOTYPE: time encoded in position rather than in stack count — a departure from pure Neurath grammar that the rubric should name and assess.
