# Bertin — 0030 History of Women's Suffrage

## School Fidelity /20 → 16

Visual variable audit:
- Position (grid order, read L-R/T-B): temporal sequence — ordered. This is the grammar innovation. Position is the strongest ordered variable; using it for temporal sequence is well-matched.
- Shape (flag-pictogram): country identity — nominal. Correct — shape is selective and associative.
- Color (border): geographic region — nominal. Correct — hue is selective/associative for nominal data.
- Size: fixed — country type is not encoded by size. Correct for ISOTYPE (no scaling).
- Decade density within grid: cluster size — quantitative (approximate). Correct — spatial density is an ordered/quantitative variable.

No grammatical errors. The position-as-time innovation is the most interesting variable choice: it uses the viewer's natural left-to-right reading direction as a temporal encoding without requiring explicit positional instruction. This is retinally efficient.

The potential grammar issue: color (hue) encodes region, but each flag also has its own colors (not encoding any data). Two color systems exist simultaneously in each pictogram — the flag's inherent colors and the border's region code. This is a layering challenge that the viewer must parse. The border solution (thin frame vs. fill) successfully separates the two color systems.

## Data/Story Integrity /20 → 16

Accurate historical dates, appropriate selection. Australia 1902 asterisk needed. The temporal sequence claim (position = adoption order) is verified by the year labels — the labels provide a second channel for the same temporal encoding, which adds verification without redundancy.

## Legibility /15 → 12

The two-color-system challenge (flag colors + region border) is the primary legibility concern. For blue-flag countries, the deep-navy region border will be difficult to distinguish from the flag's own blue at small sizes. A different border treatment (texture, double-line, or a more distinctly different color for the European region) would resolve this.

## Visual Economy /15 → 12

Fifty symbols plus two simultaneous color systems plus year labels plus country names plus decade dividers plus legend. This is at the high end of what I consider economical for this format. Each element is doing real work, but the cumulative visual load is significant. For an ISOTYPE work designed for democratic accessibility, this may be too complex for the intended public-display context.

## Resonance /15 → 12

The temporal argument is correctly encoded. The isolation effect is powerful. The regional clustering patterns (the 1940s decolonization wave shows a mix of region colors) are available for the patient viewer.

## Craft /15 → 13

The flag simplification requires high craft. The border treatment must be precisely consistent across all 50 pictograms.

## My Total: 81/100

A sound ISOTYPE work with a genuine grammar innovation. The two simultaneous color systems are the main design risk. The temporal position encoding is correctly matched to the data type. The grammar innovation (temporal ISOTYPE) should be documented in the ISOTYPE school file.
