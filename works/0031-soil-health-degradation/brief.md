# Brief — 0031 Soil Health Degradation

## The Story

Agricultural soil degradation is one of the least-visible environmental crises — it happens underground, over decades, in a dimension (depth) that cannot be directly observed. Four soil health variables (pH, organic matter content, compaction, microbial diversity) measured at five depths across eight global agricultural regions reveal the crisis. The argument: the topsoil layer (0-10cm) is degrading fastest, threatening the agricultural foundation of global food security.

## Form

8 side-by-side soil profile cross-sections. Depth as primary vertical axis (down = deeper, inverting the usual visualization convention). Each of the 8 columns is one region's soil profile; each profile shows color-coded zones for the 4 soil health variables as bands within the profile. A separate small panel shows the environmental variable gradients (light, temperature, moisture) at each depth.

## Innovation Target

Axis inversion (depth axis runs downward instead of upward like altitude) and its implications for ecological visualization grammar. Humboldt applied altitude going up; soil science requires depth going down. The axis inversion is not a design choice — it is a grammar requirement imposed by the subject matter.
