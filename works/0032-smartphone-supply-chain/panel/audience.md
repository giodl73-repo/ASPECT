# Audience Lens — 0032 Global Smartphone Supply Chain

## School Fidelity /20 → 15

I am a policy analyst at a technology trade organization. I read supply chain reports regularly but do not typically see schematic diagram formats applied to supply chains — I usually see flow charts, maps, or tables. The stage column structure is immediately intuitive: EXTRACTION → PROCESSING → COMPONENTS → ASSEMBLY → MARKET reads like a standard supply chain framework I know.

The material color lines are clear. I can follow the cobalt line (orange) from the DRC through Belgium/China processing through battery component manufacturing through assembly. This flow tracking is exactly the information I need for a risk assessment.

The red chokepoint nodes are the first thing I see after the stage headers. I immediately know: these are the nodes I need to pay attention to. The material count inside each node (e.g., "5" inside China's processing node, meaning 5 of 6 materials flow through here) gives me the quantitative dimension I need to prioritize.

## Data/Story Integrity /20 → 16

The topology is accurate based on my prior knowledge. The China processing dominance is well-documented. The Taiwan semiconductor chokepoint is real. The sources are credible.

The date stamp issue (rapid changes in lithium processing) is something I am aware of professionally. A 2023 date stamp is essential for this kind of diagram.

## Legibility /15 → 14

For my audience: highly legible. The stage framework is familiar; the material colors are distinguishable; the chokepoints are immediately visible. I can answer "where are the supply chain risks?" in under 10 seconds.

For a general audience without supply chain context: the stage column structure would need introduction. The word "chokepoint" would need definition. But for my professional audience, no introduction needed.

## Visual Economy /15 → 14

Everything in this diagram is earning its place. The material count inside nodes is particularly efficient — I immediately know a node with "5" is more critical than a node with "1" without counting lines.

## Resonance /15 → 14

This diagram gives me the strategic picture I have been trying to assemble from multiple reports. Seeing all six materials' flows in one diagram reveals patterns I could not see from reading separate material-specific analyses: the convergence at the processing stage is more severe than I realized across all six materials simultaneously. That realization — a genuine "aha" moment — is the work's strongest resonance achievement.

## Craft /15 → 14

Clean, professional, would appear in a government or industry report without modification.

## My Total: 87/100

The strongest policy-facing visualization in the project for a technical audience. The stage-anchor structure is exactly the right organizational framework for supply chain analysis. The convergence pattern makes the risk argument immediately visible.
