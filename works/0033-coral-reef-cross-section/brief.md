# Brief — 0033 Coral Reef Cross-Section

## The Story

A coral reef is the ocean's most biodiverse ecosystem per unit area — more species per square meter than any terrestrial ecosystem. But its biodiversity distributes vertically: different species occupy different depth zones based on light, temperature, and pressure. A cross-section from surface to the mesophotic zone (60m) reveals three distinct ecological communities that never meet. The crisis: shallow-water coral bleaching (from warming) is already destroying the 0-10m community; the mid-reef is at risk; only the deep mesophotic zone provides a potential refuge.

## Design Approach

A vertical cross-section of a coral reef, surface (0m) to mesophotic zone (60m). The visual field is filled with accurately rendered organisms at the depth where each species lives — this is the Haeckel grammar applied to ecological zonation rather than single-taxon morphology. The visual density is the data: the species distribution creates compositional rhythm.

## Innovation Target

Ecology-as-composition: when the scientific subject is an ecosystem, the species distribution creates compositional rhythm distinct from Haeckel's single-organism plates. The rubric's scientific illustration school should name this.
