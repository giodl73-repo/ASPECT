# Design Specification — 0033 Coral Reef Cross-Section

## Canvas

Portrait orientation, 60cm x 90cm. The primary image is a vertical cross-section from water surface (top) to mesophotic zone bottom (60m). A narrow right-margin panel (10cm wide) shows the three environmental variables (light intensity, temperature, carbonate saturation) as depth-scaled graphs.

## Depth Zones and Species

The cross-section is divided into three depth zones, each with a distinct ecological community and visual character:

### Zone 1: Surface Zone (0-10m)
Visual character: bright, dappled light from above. Water is clear blue-green. Dominant organisms:
- Branching Acropora corals (2-3 large colonies, foreground)
- Parrotfish (Scarus species, 2-3 individuals)
- Sea turtle (1, Chelonia mydas)
- Dense school of surgeonfish (Acanthurus species, 20+ individuals as a collective shape)
- Sea fan (Gorgonia species, background)
- Soft coral beds (Alcyonaria, mid-ground)

### Zone 2: Mid-Reef (10-30m)
Visual character: dimmer, more diffuse light. Water transitions to deeper blue. Dominant organisms:
- Plate corals (Montipora species, horizontal plates 3-5 in layered arrangement)
- Grouper (Epinephelus species, 1-2 individuals)
- Spotted eagle ray (Aetobatus narinari, 1)
- Octopus (Octopus vulgaris, 1, semi-hidden in coral structure)
- Encrusting sponge colonies (multiple species, background color patches)
- Wire coral (Cirrhipathes species, begins to appear at lower zone boundary)

### Zone 3: Mesophotic Zone (30-60m)
Visual character: dim, greenish-blue light. Almost no sunlight penetration. Dominant organisms:
- Cup corals (Leptoseris species, solitary, scattered)
- Wire coral (Cirrhipathes species, tall vertical forms)
- Deep-water sea lily (Crinoidea, 2-3)
- Lanternfish (Myctophidae, small schooling forms)
- Red deepwater shrimp (Stenopus species, several)
- Black coral (Antipatharia, 1-2 tree-form colonies)

## Depth Axis

A vertical depth scale appears on the left edge of the image: 0m (surface, top), 10m (zone 1-2 boundary), 30m (zone 2-3 boundary), 60m (bottom). The scale is labeled in meters with small tick marks and a continuous vertical line. The zone boundaries are marked with thin horizontal dashed lines across the image, with zone labels: "EUPHOTIC ZONE (0-10m)" / "MESOPHOTIC TRANSITION (10-30m)" / "MESOPHOTIC ZONE (30-60m)".

## Visual Density Strategy (Ecology-as-Composition)

The visual density in each zone reflects the ecological reality:
- Zone 1 (surface): densest — highest species diversity, most light, most visual complexity. The Acropora branching structures fill the foreground; the fish school creates a unified visual mass; color is warmest (blues, greens, oranges of fish coloration).
- Zone 2 (mid-reef): medium density — plate coral creates horizontal structure; fewer fish but larger individuals; color deepens (more blue, less warm tone).
- Zone 3 (mesophotic): sparsest — few organisms widely spaced; wire coral creates vertical linear elements against nearly empty water column; color is cool (deep blue-green, near black at bottom edge).

This density gradient — dense-to-sparse from top to bottom — is the ecological argument made visible as composition. The viewer feels the zone boundaries as compositional shifts: the eye slows as the visual field empties. The mesophotic zone's sparseness is data, not aesthetic choice.

## Haeckel Grammar Application

Haeckel's scientific illustration grammar:
- Morphological accuracy: each species rendered at accurate proportions for its depth zone
- Ecological truth: each organism placed at the depth where it actually lives
- Visual density as data: the density of organisms encodes the ecological richness of each zone
- Intrinsic complexity: the complexity is the subject matter, not decoration

This work extends Haeckel's grammar from single-taxon plates (one species per plate, showing morphological variation) to multi-taxon ecological scenes (multiple species per plate, showing ecological community structure). The extension is justified: Haeckel's Kunstformen der Natur plates occasionally showed ecological groupings alongside individual morphological studies.

## Environmental Variable Panel (Right Margin)

Three depth-scaled line graphs, each 3cm wide x 75cm tall, arranged horizontally:
1. Light intensity (lux, 0-10m range 2000-50,000; mesophotic 1-50)
2. Temperature (Celsius, 0m: 28C; 60m: 24C, showing thermocline at 15-20m)
3. Carbonate saturation (aragonite saturation state, 0m: 4.0; 60m: 3.2, declining with depth)

Each graph uses the same downward depth axis as the main cross-section, allowing direct alignment between organism position and environmental variable value. The graphs are small and intended for close reading — they provide the scientific quantitative layer for the viewer who wants to understand the mechanism behind the distributional pattern.

Scientific illustration (Haeckel grammar extended to ecological scenes):
- Morphological accuracy throughout
- Ecological placement accuracy: each organism at its documented depth zone

- Extension of grammar: Haeckel's single-taxon plates → multi-taxon ecological scene (named innovation)
