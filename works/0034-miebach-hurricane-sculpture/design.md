# Design Notes — 0034 Hurricane Noel Data Sculpture

## Material Grammar

The basket is woven from reed and wire over a roughly circular frame. The frame is divided into 36 radial positions (one per 10 degrees of compass bearing), each position corresponding to a wave-height measurement at that bearing during the storm's peak passage. The basket wall rises and falls around this frame, its height at each position directly proportional to wave height in meters.

## Encoding Key (companion documentation)

| Variable | Encoding | Range |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Temperature (sea surface) | Color (red-white-blue gradient) | 24°C–28°C |
| Barometric pressure | Weave density (strands per cm) | 945–1012 mbar |
| Wave height | Wall height (cm) | 0.5–7.3 m |
| Wind speed events | Pattern interruption (crossweave) | > 50 knot threshold |

## Structural Decisions

**Circular plan form**: The hurricane's roughly circular structure — eye at center, bands radiating outward — is preserved in the plan geometry. A viewer looking down at the object sees the storm's radial organization without being told to see it.

**Reading direction**: The basket is read clockwise from the north position, following the storm's chronological progression as the eye moved north-northeast over October 29–November 1.

**Weave density as pressure**: Tight weave is physically harder to achieve than loose weave — the increased labor of tight weaving creates a tactile correspondence with the atmospheric compression of low pressure. This is not purely visual encoding; it is material-haptic encoding.

**Companion documentation**: A printed key provides the encoding table and a timeline of the storm passage. Without the key, the object functions as sculpture. With the key, it functions as data visualization. This dual mode is deliberate — Miebach's work is intended for gallery and research contexts, where the key is available.

## Dual-Mode Reading

The work functions in two registers simultaneously:
1. **Aesthetic mode**: the object has visual rhythm, color variation, and structural interest as pure form — approachable before the encoding is known
2. **Data mode**: with the encoding key, every variation in height, density, color, and pattern becomes a specific measurement from a specific moment in the storm

This dual-mode structure is intrinsic to Miebach's data-art practice and should not be scored as a legibility failure. The aesthetic layer is not noise; it is the first layer of a two-layer reading.
