# Design Notes — 0036 NYT Capitol Attack

## Form Sequence

The work is a scroll narrative published on NYT.com. The reader scrolls down; the visual forms change. Each form serves a specific explanatory function:

**Form 1 — Satellite imagery**: Overhead view of the Capitol and grounds at 2:30 PM on January 6. Crowd size annotations. Function: establish the spatial scale of the assembly and the moment of maximum convergence on the building.

**Form 2 — Annotated architectural floor plans**: Capitol floor plan with breach points marked, movement paths drawn, and specific doors labeled by time of breach. Function: establish the building's internal geography; allow the reader to understand where events occurred in relation to the chambers and offices.

**Form 3 — Video stills with movement paths**: Individual frames from surveillance footage, open-source video, and press footage, with colored paths overlaid showing the movement of identifiable individuals. Function: place specific people in specific corridors at specific times; establish individual accountability within the spatial narrative.

**Form 4 — 3D corridor reconstructions**: Photogrammetric reconstructions of key corridors (Senate wing hallway, Rotunda, Speaker's lobby). Function: give spatial depth and orientation that 2D floor plans cannot — the reader understands height, width, and the physical experience of the spaces.

**Form 5 — Timeline charts**: Horizontal timeline showing key events from noon to 5 PM, with vertical lines indicating when each floor plan location was breached. Function: place the spatial narrative in temporal sequence; show the cascade from one breach to the next.

## Coherence Test

Each form transition is tested against the question: does the new form answer a question the previous form raised but could not resolve?

- Satellite → Floor plan: "I can see the crowd reached the building — but where exactly did they enter, and where did they go inside?" Floor plan answers this.
- Floor plan → Video stills: "I can see the paths were through these corridors — but who was in them, and when?" Video stills answer this.
- Video stills → 3D reconstruction: "I can see individuals in corridors, but I cannot feel the spatial experience — how tight were these spaces, where were the defenders, what did it look like?" 3D answers this.
- 3D → Timeline: "I understand the spatial events — but what is the temporal order? Which breach led to which?" Timeline answers this.

Each transition is motivated by an unanswered question from the previous form. This is the Cluster R test: multi-form narrative coherence as a design criterion.
