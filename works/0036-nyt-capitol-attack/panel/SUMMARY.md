## Key Agreements

**Motivated form transitions are the coherence criterion.** All five reviewers agree: each of the five form transitions in this scroll narrative is motivated by a question the previous form raised but could not resolve. Satellite → floor plan → video stills → 3D → timeline. Each transition feels necessary rather than arbitrary. This is the Cluster R criterion generalized from work 0027: multi-form narrative coherence is achieved when every form transition answers a question the previous form raised.

**Purpose scores the highest in this panel.** Du Bois gives 15/15; all other reviewers give 14/15. The accountability journalism purpose — constituting a complete visual record of a historically unprecedented event — is served at the highest level. The resonance is proportionate to the historical significance.

**Legibility friction is real but managed.** All reviewers note cognitive re-orientation cost from five form transitions. The text bridges between forms manage this friction adequately. The Audience reviewer's experience (25 minutes, some fatigue by Form 5) quantifies the cost. The friction is justified by the informational necessity of each form.

## CLUSTER R — CONFIRMED ACROSS WORKS 0027 + 0036

**Cluster R: Multi-form narrative coherence** is confirmed across two works.

- **Work 0027** (#207 Nightingale, #210 Tufte): multi-form narrative coherence was first identified in the green space inequality scroll narrative — the cross-section visual continuity across sections and the multi-form coherence as a fidelity criterion.
- **Work 0036** (#241 Nightingale, and confirmed by all five reviewers): the same coherence criterion is confirmed in the Capitol attack scroll narrative, a more complex work with five distinct visual grammars.

**The confirmed coherence criterion:** Multi-form narrative coherence is achieved when every form transition in a scroll narrative is motivated by a question the previous form raised but could not resolve. The criterion is testable: for each transition in the narrative, ask "What question did the previous form raise that this new form answers?" If the question exists and the new form answers it, the transition is motivated and coherent. If no question was raised, or the new form does not resolve it, the transition is arbitrary and coherence fails.

**The v2.0 Purpose dimension captures part of this question but not all of it.** Purpose asks: does the form serve the function? For multi-form narrative, this question must be asked at each transition, not only at the level of the whole work. The Legibility modifier for dynamic/interactive works (currently in v2.0) applies to time-animation and interaction but does not name the multi-form coherence criterion specifically.

**Amendment recommendation:** Add to School Fidelity dimension, under Type A anchor for narrative-visualization:

> *For multi-form narratives (scroll narratives using two or more distinct visual grammars in sequence):* apply the coherence test at each form transition. Score full fidelity (18–20) only if every transition is motivated — each new form answers a question the previous form raised but could not resolve. Unmotivated transitions (form changes for visual variety, not informational necessity) are fidelity failures. Note the count of motivated vs. unmotivated transitions in the review.

**Status: CLUSTER R CONFIRMED — two works, three reviewer instances in 0027, five reviewer instances in 0036, rubric amendment identified.**
