# Brief — 0039 Boreal Forest Vertical Cross-Section

## Subject

A vertical cross-section of a boreal (taiga) forest, reading from canopy peak to root zone — the full ecological column organized on a single gravitational axis. The subject is the boreal biome: the largest terrestrial biome on Earth, covering Canada, Russia, Scandinavia, and northern Alaska.

## Four Ecological Zones

| Zone | Depth | Key Species |
|------|-------|-------------|
| Canopy zone | 15–30m | Black spruce (*Picea mariana*), white spruce (*Picea glauca*), jack pine (*Pinus banksiana*), balsam fir (*Abies balsamea*) |
| Understory zone | 5–15m | Balsam fir regeneration, speckled alder (*Alnus incana*), mountain ash (*Sorbus americana*), young white spruce |
| Shrub/ground layer | 0–5m | Labrador tea (*Rhododendron groenlandicum*), lingonberry (*Vaccinium vitis-idaea*), sphagnum moss, bracken fern, cloudberry |
| Root zone | 0 to −2m | Black spruce tap roots, lateral root mats (balsam fir), mycorrhizal networks (ectomycorrhizal and arbuscular), soil fauna (nematodes, Collembola, earthworms scarce), permafrost boundary at −1.5m |

## Design Question

Does the honest rendering of species at their true ecological positions — dense crown layer with sparse canopy gaps, irregular understory, packed ground layer, dense root mat — create a compositional structure where the visual density gradient IS the ecological data, not decoration?

This is the Cluster W confirmation question: the boreal column should exhibit the same ecology-as-composition dynamics previously documented in coral reef (0033) and ocean depth (0038) contexts.

## Environmental Variables (Flanking Columns)

Four flanking columns, each calibrated against the same vertical axis:

1. **Light intensity** (lux): full sun above canopy (~50,000 lux) → below-canopy dappled (~200–800 lux) → near-ground (~20–100 lux) → subterranean (0)
2. **Temperature range** (°C): recorded daily range; large swings in canopy (−40° to +30° annual range, diurnal swings of 15°C+) → narrowing in understory → minimal variance at ground level → near-constant near-freezing in root zone
3. **Moisture content** (%) of immediate environment: moderate in canopy → moderate in understory → very high at ground (sphagnum holds 90%+ water) → saturated at permafrost boundary
4. **Soil organic matter** (%): minimal in canopy → builds from needle fall at understory → high in upper soil layer → very high in deep root zone (deep peat accumulation in boreal)

These appear as flanking columns exactly as in Humboldt's *Tableau Physique* grammar.
