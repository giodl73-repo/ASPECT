### Cluster W Canonical Statement

Ecology-as-composition: When organisms are placed at their true ecological positions in a cross-section illustration, the visual density and compositional rhythm that emerge from truthful placement will constitute the ecological argument. This is not decoration; it is the data made visible as form. The principle is ecosystem-independent: it holds in coral reef, ocean depth, and boreal forest contexts, producing different compositional signatures for each ecosystem's actual vertical organization.

## Key Agreements

**The density rhythm is the work's primary achievement.** All five reviewers identify the DENSE to SPARSE to DENSE to DENSE pattern as the correct ecological representation of the boreal column. Bertin names it as a visual grammar element; Tufte accepts it as intrinsically necessary; Artist identifies it as resonance mechanism; Historian confirms it as the W-cluster principle generalized; Audience describes a discovery experience structured by density changes.

**The permafrost line is the work's most powerful single element.** Tufte, Artist, and Audience all independently identify the permafrost boundary as the most ecologically and compositionally significant element. The hard visual stop at -1.5m communicates an absolute physical constraint that determines the architecture of the entire column above it.

**Source declaration is adequate.** Unlike work 0033, this work cites specific authoritative sources. Direct application of the lesson from 0033's primary gap.
