## Key Agreements

**The sorting failure is the central problem.** All five reviewers independently flag alphabetical country ordering as the most damaging single decision. Playfair, Tufte, Bertin: it is a grammar violation for a school whose power is comparative clarity through organization. Du Bois: it conceals the structural chasm between income/stewardship tiers.

**The palette is technically correct but argumentatively inert.** Sequential red encodes magnitude correctly but does not encode deviation from achievable rates. A diverging palette anchored at WHO stewardship targets would make the argument visible in the color encoding.

**Integrity is the strongest dimension.** Source declarations, missing data treatment, and income tier disclosure are all handled correctly. The statistician's surveillance comparability concern is the primary Integrity gap.

## Top 3 Recommendations for Cycle 2

1. **Sort countries by income tier then stewardship classification** — single change with largest score impact across School Fidelity, Legibility, Resonance, Purpose simultaneously. All five reviewers agree this is the primary gap.

2. **Diverging color palette anchored at WHO-stewardship target rates** — encode the deviation argument in the color structure, not just magnitude. Converts the form from information display to advocacy structure.

3. **Add surveillance comparability note and uncertainty indicators** — addresses the Statistician and Du Bois Integrity concerns without changing the form's argument. Essential for a policy audience that will scrutinize data quality.

## Innovations from Cycle 1

**Innovation #264 — Matrix reordering as advocacy prerequisite:** In a statistical-graphics heat matrix designed for advocacy, sorting rows by the argument's structural variable (income tier/stewardship quality) is not optional -- it is the mechanism by which the advocacy argument becomes visible. An unsorted advocacy matrix is a policy argument that refuses to state itself. The rubric correctly penalizes this in School Fidelity (grammar violation) and Purpose (form does not serve function).

**Innovation #265 — Diverging palette as argument structure:** When the advocacy argument is "deviation from an achievable standard," a diverging color palette anchored at the achievable standard makes the argument visible in the color encoding itself. A sequential palette encodes magnitude (how much) but not deviation (how far from good). The choice between sequential and diverging is an argument choice, not only a craft choice.
