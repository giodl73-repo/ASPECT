## Innovations from Cycle 2

**Innovation #266 -- Income tier as matrix sort key in global health advocacy:** When the advocacy argument is structural (income/stewardship quality determines outcomes), sorting the matrix by the structural variable converts information display into argument structure. The sort order is itself an editorial statement: I am telling you what the organizing variable is by showing it to you in the organization.

**Innovation #267 -- Diverging palette anchored at achievable standard:** For advocacy matrices where the argument is "deviation from achievable good practice," a diverging palette (blue = at/below standard; red = above standard) encodes the normative argument directly in the color structure. This is distinguishable from Cluster T (counterfactual fill area) by being a static encoding applied per-cell rather than a temporal or comparative area fill. A distinct structural pattern in advocacy statistical graphics.
