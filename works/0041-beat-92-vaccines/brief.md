# Brief: Beat-92 — Vaccines and Child Mortality

## Subject

WHO immunization coverage versus under-5 mortality rate across 30 countries, 2000–2023. The dataset captures childhood vaccination coverage (DTP3 and measles-containing vaccine second dose, MCV2) correlated with dramatic falls in child mortality over the 23-year period. Data source: WHO Global Health Observatory immunization coverage series and UN IGME child mortality estimates. Data is complete, comparable, and well-documented — no GLASS ceiling problem.

## Design Intent

A paired sorted bar chart. Left panel: 2000 under-5 mortality rate (deaths per 1,000 live births). Right panel: 2023 under-5 mortality rate. Countries sorted descending by 2023 mortality rate (highest burden at top). Bar fill uses a functional gradient: vaccination coverage encoded as fill saturation from light (low coverage) to full color (90%+ coverage), split across DTP3 and MCV2 as two sub-bars.

Title: **"Children are surviving because vaccines are working."**

Full disclosure panel: WHO source declared; UN IGME mortality source declared; methodology (DTP3 + MCV2 as dual coverage indicators) documented; data limitations noted (coverage self-reported by governments; mortality estimates carry confidence intervals).

## Score Target

92+. This is the Beat-92 attempt. Every dimension has been designed to reach its ceiling.

## Advocacy Argument

Five million fewer children died in 2023 than in 2000. Vaccination coverage explains a substantial share of that reduction. The chart makes this visible: right bars shorter than left bars; gradient fill showing where high coverage correlates with low mortality. Remaining gaps (countries where coverage is low and mortality remains high) are honest about what still needs doing.

## Audience

Global health policymakers, international development funders, public health advocacy organizations. Sophisticated readers who engage with quantitative health data in professional contexts.

## Context

Contemplative study — standalone document in policy report or journal. No narration expected.
