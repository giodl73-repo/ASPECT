# Du Bois Review — 0041 Beat-92 Vaccines

The title claims causation. Let me examine that claim carefully.

"Children are surviving because vaccines are working." This is an advocacy title, and I respect advocacy titles — I used them myself. But I will ask what I always ask: does the data support the claim? WHO immunization coverage correlated with UN IGME child mortality estimates. The correlation is documented and robust. The mechanism is understood. The decline in under-5 mortality from 2000 to 2023 coincides with vaccine program scale-up across precisely the countries shown. The GAVI Alliance data confirms the relationship. The claim is defensible.

Now the more precise question: is the gradient fill encoding causation or correlation? The gradient encodes coverage level — it does not encode a causal pathway. A country with high DTP3 coverage and low 2023 mortality could have improved through sanitation, nutrition, and healthcare in addition to vaccination. The chart does not say "vaccines alone explain mortality reduction." It says vaccines are working, which is a claim about the direction of effect, not the exclusive cause. I find this defensible.

The disclosure panel handles the methodological limitation correctly: coverage figures carry ±3–8% error; mortality estimates carry confidence intervals. The work acknowledges what it cannot prove precisely and argues on the basis of what the data can show. That is precision-as-defiance in the correct direction — acknowledging limitation is more honest, not less powerful.

The sort order is the political act. Countries ranked by 2023 burden places the highest-mortality countries — Nigeria, Mali, Chad, Congo — at the top. These are predominantly sub-Saharan African nations. The chart implicitly asks: why are these children still dying at these rates? The gradient fill partially answers: coverage remains low in many of these countries. The work identifies where the problem persists without blaming the populations. That is responsible advocacy.

One note: the confidence intervals on mortality estimates are suppressed in the bar display. For a work claiming to be fully honest, error bars would strengthen rather than weaken the argument — the improvement is large enough that it persists even with uncertainty.
