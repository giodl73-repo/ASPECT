# Playfair Review — 0041 Beat-92 Vaccines

Here is a bar chart. A proper bar chart. You have the 2000 bars on the left, the 2023 bars on the right, countries sorted by the right-hand values, and the argument jumps off the page before you have read the title. Nigeria: 203 children per thousand in 2000; 72 in 2023. Mali: 218 to 91. The bars shrink dramatically across the entire sorted list. That is the comparison. I invented this form precisely because it would do this — make comparison so immediate that the viewer grasps the argument before they have formed a sentence. This work does exactly that.

The gradient fill encoding vaccination coverage inside the bar itself is the cleverest element. A weaker designer would have put vaccine coverage in a separate chart. Here it is inside the bar — you see the mortality, and simultaneously you see whether vaccines were reaching these children. The double sub-bar (DTP3 blue, MCV2 teal) is a small complexity, but one that pays its way immediately. Countries with pale fills still have high mortality on the right side. Countries with saturated fills have shorter right bars. The correlation is visible without a regression line.

The title — "Children are surviving because vaccines are working" — does what I always said a chart title should do: it states the argument, not merely the content. It does not say "Under-5 Mortality 2000–2023." It says what you should see and why. That is correct. That is how charts reach people who do not arrive already persuaded.

I would strengthen the sort. Sorting by 2023 mortality is right for the advocacy argument. But a secondary disclosure — "countries ranked by 2023 burden" — should appear more prominently. The methodology note is buried in small text. Bring it to the subtitle, where it belongs.
