# Rosling Review — 0041 Beat-92 Vaccines

Finally. Someone who is willing to show the improvement.

I spent years watching people carry a fixed picture of the world in their heads — a picture formed sometime around 1980 and never updated. They believe child mortality is still at crisis levels across Africa. They do not know that Nigeria went from 203 to 72 per thousand. They do not know that Sub-Saharan Africa as a whole has halved its child mortality rate in 23 years. The world has changed dramatically, and most people have not been told.

This chart tells them.

The sorted bar chart with the two-panel comparison is the right form for this argument. Left panel: 2000. Right panel: 2023. Countries in the same order in both panels. The eye can follow each country from left to right and see the bar shrink. The total gestalt — all the right bars are shorter than all the left bars — is available before you read a single number. That is the strength of this form. I would have used animation, yes, but I will admit this static version has a power that animation can dilute: you see 2000 and 2023 simultaneously, both registering at once.

My one push: I want to see where these countries are going. The 2023 bar is shorter than the 2000 bar, excellent. But is the rate of improvement accelerating or decelerating? Is the pace of vaccine scale-up keeping up with population growth? A small inset trend line per country would add a third argument: the trajectory, not just the state. This is not a defect of the current design — it is a suggestion for version two. The current design is complete as advocacy.

The gradient encoding vaccination coverage is brilliant. You do not need to look up vaccination data separately. It is inside the mortality bar. The mechanism is visible in the data.
