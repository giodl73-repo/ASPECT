## Dimension Analysis

**School Fidelity (17.8/20):** Dynamic-statistical-graphics grammar fully applied. Time-as-animation is the correct primary encoding for temporal argument. The color choice — blue-to-red gradient — is the one border case: it is technically correct (ordered variable for ordered data) but connotative (thermal association). All reviewers accepted the encoding; Tufte scored it lowest at 17.

**Integrity (17.85/20):** NASA GISS data declared; baseline (1951-1980) declared. No causal overclaim — the animation shows the anomaly trend; narration does the advocacy work. Audience reviewer noted baseline label could be more prominent.

**Legibility (12.8/15):** Accompanied context (narrated presentation) is the primary mode; [static: 10-11/15]. The dynamic experience is well-served; the narration carries context for historical inflection points that the frame cannot provide alone.

**Execution (13.5/15):** Per-frame economy near-perfect. Animation scored as whole, not frame-by-frame. Key innovation (#273): the animation is the unit of evaluation; frames are its marks. Trailing 10-year line adds context without cluttering the primary bar.

**Resonance (13.45/15):** Experiential resonance genuine — the temporal accumulation of watching 144 years pass creates weight that no static chart produces. Rosling scored 14/15; audience scored 14/15. Tufte noted the resonance is partially narrator-dependent.

**Purpose (12.65/15):** Climate advocacy through temporal experience is a valid purpose. The gap: no explicit action frame converts the experience into a directive. The animation ends; the viewer has experienced something alarming but received no guidance on what it means or what can be done. This is the design's primary unresolved tension.

## Top 3 Design Recommendations

1. Elevate baseline label to subtitle — "Anomaly relative to 1951-1980 average" belongs in the subtitle, not small text on the zero-line. This answers the skeptic's first question before they ask it.
2. Add a final hold frame with one-sentence action text — after the 2024 frame, hold for 5 seconds with: "2024: +1.45 degrees C. Every tenth of a degree matters." Converts experience into advocacy.
3. Add 10-year running average line as a persistent overlay — helps viewers distinguish signal from noise in early frames; makes the contrast between flat (1880-1960) and rising (1980-2024) visually explicit without adding complexity.

## Innovation Logged

**#273 — Animation as unit of evaluation:** For animated visualizations, the animation is the scoring unit; individual frames are its marks. Scoring frames individually would penalize parsimony — the frames are nearly empty because the animation is the encoding. This is analogous to scoring a sparkline by ink strokes rather than by the whole data series. Rule: score the dynamic experience as primary; note static legibility in brackets.
