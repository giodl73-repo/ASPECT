# Audience Review — 0042 Temperature Anomalies

I understand this immediately. The bar gets bigger and turns red. I have been watching for thirty seconds and the bar has been growing steadily since around 1980. By 2024 it is bright red, tall, and the number at the top says +1.45 degrees C. I know that is bad.

The year in the corner is the best design decision. It keeps me oriented in time without asking me to look away from the bar. The trailing line behind the current bar is a nice touch — it reminds me where we just came from without making the screen complicated.

Two honest questions. First: what is the baseline? The chart says "1951–1980 average" in small text near the zero-line. The answer to "warmer than what?" should be in the subtitle, prominent. Second: what should I do? The chart shows me something alarming but doesn't tell me what it means for my life or what anyone can do. The experience ends when the animation ends. A final frame with a brief statement could convert the experience into action.

These are not design failures — they are gaps in the advocacy wrapper. The visualization itself is clear, honest, and genuinely powerful.
