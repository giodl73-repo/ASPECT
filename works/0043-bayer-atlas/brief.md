# Brief: Herbert Bayer World Geo-Graphic Atlas (1953)

## Subject

The "Population" spread from Herbert Bayer's World Geo-Graphic Atlas (1953, Container Corporation of America). World population distribution shown using isotype-adjacent pictographic density maps plus statistical bar charts. The spread integrates geographic density, regional population totals, demographic pyramids, and urban/rural breakdowns in a unified Bauhaus-designed composition.

## Historical Context

Herbert Bayer (1900-1985) was a Bauhaus-trained designer who worked with Container Corporation of America from 1946. The Atlas was produced as a prestige publication demonstrating that information design could meet Bauhaus formal standards. It was designed for executives and policy professionals, not mass audiences.

## School Attribution

Information-architecture + statistical-graphics (Type C deliberate synthesis). The Atlas deliberately integrates two school grammars: information-architecture (multi-layer hierarchy, navigation, system-level composition) and statistical-graphics (bar charts, area charts, quantitative encoding). The Bauhaus aesthetic tradition adds a third layer: formal beauty as design principle.

## Score Target

82-87. The Atlas is technically accomplished but Bauhaus decorative elements create Visual Economy tension. Type C synthesis not Type B founding.
