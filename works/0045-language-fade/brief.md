# Brief: Language Endangerment as Visual Fade

## Subject

UNESCO Atlas of the World's Languages in Danger (3rd ed., 2010). 7,168 living languages; UNESCO estimates 3,000+ will die by 2100. Each language is a mark; mark opacity and size encode endangerment level. Safe languages: full opacity, full size. Severely endangered: reduced opacity, reduced size. Critically endangered: near-invisible. Already extinct languages visible only as the barest trace, positioned where they were spoken.

## Design Intent

A field of 7,168 marks arranged by geographic region. The visual effect: large blank spaces where languages have gone silent; a field already partly missing. Absence is encoded as data. The work asks: can visual absence represent real absence without being chartjunk?

## School

Data-art (Type A). The aesthetic is intentional — the work makes an argument through visual experience (fading field) rather than through statistical display.

## Score Target

80-86. The key question: absence-as-encoding — is the deliberate fading of data marks a legitimate encoding technique or decorative removal of data?
