# Design Description: Amazon Deforestation Cross-Section

## Visual Structure

**Three-panel vertical cross-section:**
Three panels left to right, each showing the same 50m vertical extent at a specific date. Vertical axis: elevation from −5m (root zone) to +45m (emergent canopy). Horizontal axis: a 100m transect.

**Panel 1 — 1980 (Primary Forest):**
- Emergent canopy layer (35–45m): massive trees with spreading crowns; epiphytic bromeliads, orchids
- Canopy layer (20–35m): dense, interlocked crowns; main light interception zone
- Understory (10–20m): shade-adapted palms, small trees, lianas
- Shrub layer (2–10m): juvenile trees, shrubs, ferns
- Herb layer (0–2m): dense herbaceous plants, forest floor mosses
- Root zone (−5m to 0): deep tap roots + lateral roots; mycorrhizal network
- Fauna: harpy eagle at canopy apex; howler monkeys in upper canopy; poison dart frogs at floor level; underground armadillo
- Vertical fill is dense throughout; the ecology-as-composition principle (W cluster) produces a layered density that IS the ecological argument

**Panel 2 — 2000 (Selectively Logged):**
- Emergent layer: gaps where large trees removed; some emergent trees remain
- Canopy: broken canopy; light penetrates to understory; invasive vines filling gaps
- Understory: denser due to light penetration; pioneer species
- Shrub and herb layers: present but altered species composition
- Root zone: disturbed; some root exposure at logging trails
- Fauna: harpy eagle absent; canopy monkeys reduced; edge-specialist birds appearing
- The vertical structure is recognizable but broken; the density gradient shows gaps

**Panel 3 — 2023 (Cleared Cattle Pasture):**
- One layer only: 0.5–1m grass (brachiaria species, introduced African grass)
- No trees; no stratification; no vertical complexity
- Fauna: cattle; white stork (edge specialist); fire ant mounds
- Root zone: compacted soil; dramatically reduced root depth
- The visual field is almost empty; vast vertical white space from 1m to 45m

**Environmental variables sidebar:**
Right of each panel, a small horizontal bar showing:
- Carbon stored: 1980: 300 t/ha → 2000: 180 t/ha → 2023: 12 t/ha
- Biodiversity index: 1980: 100 (reference) → 2000: 55 → 2023: 8
- Rainfall recycling: 1980: 78% → 2000: 52% → 2023: 14%
- Soil erosion: 1980: 0.5 t/yr/ha → 2000: 3.2 t/yr/ha → 2023: 24 t/yr/ha

**Typography:**
- Year labels at top of each panel
- Layer labels (right edge): "Emergent canopy," "Canopy," "Understory," etc.
- Vertical scale bar (left edge): 0m, 10m, 20m, 30m, 40m
- Source note: INPE deforestation data; AmazonFACE carbon data; IUCN biodiversity estimates

## School-Specific Elements

**Ecological-visualization:**
- Vertical cross-section with correct zone placement (gravitational hierarchy)
- Species placed at ecologically accurate heights
- Ecology-as-composition: the visual density gradient of 1980 IS the ecological argument; the near-empty 2023 panel IS the loss
- Intrinsic complexity is high (biological morphology, multi-species placement) — scored at intrinsic floor per v2.2

**Advocacy-visualization:**
- Three-panel temporal sequence shows the loss explicitly
- Environmental variable bars quantify the cost
- The argument: primary forest → selective logging → clearance is a one-way path; the reversibility is not shown because it is not the subject

## Ecology-as-Composition Note

The 1980 panel will have the highest visual density (highest mark count). The 2023 panel will be nearly empty — vast white space from the 1m grass layer to the top of the panel. This near-emptiness IS the advocacy argument. It is not chartjunk; it is the honest representation of a cleared cattle pasture compared to a primary forest. Absence-as-encoding (Work 0045) meets ecology-as-composition (Cluster W).
