# Statistician Review — 0047 Amazon Deforestation Cross-Section

Data quality assessment for the environmental variables.

Carbon stored (300 → 180 → 12 t/ha): The range for primary Amazon forest biomass is approximately 200-400 t/ha depending on forest type. The 1980 figure of 300 t/ha is within the documented range. Selectively logged forest (180 t/ha) is within the range for post-logging secondary forest (120-200 t/ha). Cattle pasture (12 t/ha) is within the range for degraded pasture on ex-forest land (8-20 t/ha). These figures are defensible.

Biodiversity index (100 → 55 → 8): The index values are expressed relative to 1980 = 100. The post-logging value (55) and post-clearance value (8) are consistent with published literature on species loss in logged and cleared Amazon sites. IUCN reports ~30-70% species loss in selectively logged sites depending on logging intensity; cleared sites lose 85-95% of original biodiversity. The index values are plausible.

Rainfall recycling (78% → 52% → 14%): Amazon forest recycling of precipitation (evapotranspiration contributing to new rainfall) is estimated at 50-80% for intact forest (consistent with 78%). Post-logging reduction is understudied but models suggest 30-60% reduction. The post-clearance figure of 14% is consistent with the documented collapse of evapotranspiration in cleared areas. The uncertainty on the 2000 figure is higher than the other two.

Soil erosion (0.5 → 3.2 → 24 t/yr/ha): Primary forest erosion rates are typically 0.1-1 t/yr/ha; the 0.5 figure is correct. Logged areas with road networks can reach 2-10 t/yr/ha; 3.2 is plausible. Compacted cattle pasture (24 t/yr/ha) is within the published range for sloped terrain (10-50 t/yr/ha). The figure depends heavily on slope and rainfall intensity; the work should declare the assumed slope conditions.

Overall data quality: good. The figures are within published ranges and from credible sources. The biodiversity index is the most model-dependent and carries the most uncertainty. A confidence range for each variable would strengthen the integrity claim.
