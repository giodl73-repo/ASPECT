**Is the Napoleon map's score inflated by emotional weight?** No. The 1.1-point gap between Napoleon (85.5) and Wine (84.4) is explained by:

**The rubric handles the commercial vs. tragedy distinction correctly through Purpose.** The Purpose dimension's moral weight proportionality criterion distinguishes "subjects with clear human stakes" from subjects without. Wine exports in 1864 are commercially important but not morally urgent. The Napoleon march is both commercially documented and morally catastrophic. The scoring difference is appropriate and not inflation.

## Dimension Analysis

**School Fidelity (18.0/20):** All three Minard maps score identically on School Fidelity. The flow map grammar is correctly applied in each; the Type C synthesis is present in each; the school-story match is equally strong.

**Integrity (18.0/20):** The wine map's highest score — contemporary commercial records, no uncertainty issues, band unit declared. This is the one dimension where the wine map scores higher than the Napoleon map (which carries uncertainty from attrition models for the Russian winter casualties).

**Legibility (13.0/15):** The multi-band convergence near France is the primary legibility issue. Multiple bands originating from the same source require tracking effort. Labels at destinations are present and correct, but the viewer must work harder than with a single-band map (Napoleon or Hannibal).

**Execution (13.45/15):** Band encoding correct. Four variables in one register is technically strong but lower dimensional than the Napoleon map's six variables in two registers. The multi-band structure is an inherent complexity of commercial flow data, not a design error.

**Resonance (10.55/15):** The significant gap from the military maps. Domain resonance for commercial geographers and trade historians; informational satisfaction for general viewers. No advocacy resonance; no experiential weight of human loss. The historian noted the historical irony (1864 = last peak before phylloxera) for informed viewers — a +0 to +1 domain resonance bonus that doesn't move the score significantly.

**Purpose (11.35/15):** Informational/commercial. The form serves the purpose: it shows trade flows in their geographic context, adding geographic dimension to a commercial comparison. The stakes are commercial not human. The rubric correctly scores this as lower-stakes than human tragedy without penalizing it for being "merely commercial."

## Top 3 Design Recommendations

1. Add temporal context note — a single line: "Note: French wine exports collapsed in 1870-1880 due to phylloxera blight; this map shows the last peak of 19th-century wine trade." This would add historical framing and raise Purpose slightly.
2. Distinguish major export routes by minor color variation — using 2-3 subtle tonal variations for the largest bands (UK, Belgium, Switzerland) would reduce the multi-band tracking difficulty without compromising the Type C synthesis.
3. Add small pie charts at major destinations — showing the proportion of French wine in each destination's total wine imports would add a third variable (French market share) and raise the information density toward Napoleon levels.
