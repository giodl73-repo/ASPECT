# Brief: Minard's Wine Export Flow Map (1864)

## Historical Work

Carte figurative et approximative des quantites de vins francais exportes en 1864. Published by Minard 1864 — five years before the Napoleon march map and two years before the Hannibal map.

## Subject

French wine exports in 1864, by destination country. Flow map with band width encoding export volume; direction encoding destination. France is the source; bands flow outward to various countries. Major destinations: UK, Belgium, Switzerland, USA, various colonial territories. No human tragedy; no emotional weight. Pure commercial cartography.

## Comparison Required

Compare to 0001 (Napoleon march, 85.5) and 0046 (Hannibal, 85.1). Minard reviews his own commercial work. The key question: does the Napoleon map deserve its score over the wine map? The Purpose dimension should handle this cleanly — human tragedy vs. commercial data is a genuine Purpose difference. But the technical quality (School Fidelity, Execution, Legibility) should be comparable.

## Score Target
