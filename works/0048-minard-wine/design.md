# Design Description: Minard's Wine Export Flow Map (1864)

## Visual Structure

**Geographic base:** Europe, with France at center-left. Coastlines, major country boundaries, and ocean areas. The map shows France and adjacent countries (UK, Belgium, Netherlands, Switzerland, Germany, Italy, Spain) plus connections to more distant territories (Algeria, US, various).

**Band structure:**
- Multiple flow bands radiating outward from France
- Band width proportional to wine export volume (hectoliters or equivalent)
- Direction: outward from France to destination
- Each major destination has a separate labeled band
- Color: single color (tan/ochre) for all export flows — direction is encoded by band origin/terminus, not by color change
- Key export flows labeled with quantity at the receiving end

**Scale:** Minard declares the band width unit (e.g., "une ligne = X hectoliters")

**Text elements:**
- Title block with date (1864 vintage exports)
- Quantity labels at major destinations
- Place name labels at geographic positions
- Scale declaration

## Key Differences from Napoleon and Hannibal Maps

| Feature | Napoleon | Hannibal | Wine |
|---------|----------|----------|------|

| Human stakes | 400,000+ deaths | 70,000+ deaths | Economic activity |
| Emotional weight | Very high | High | Low |
| Purpose dimension | Advocacy/documentary | Historical | Informational/commercial |
| Registers | 2 (march + temperature) | 1 | 1 |
| Directionality | Bidirectional (advance + retreat) | Unidirectional | Unidirectional (France outward) |
| Data quality | Contemporary military records | Ancient sources | Contemporary commercial records |
| Uncertainty | Low | High | Low |
| Moral weight | Catastrophic | Historical | None |

## School-Specific Elements

The wine map applies Minard's flow map grammar exactly — band width = quantity, direction = flow direction, geographic path = real geographic route (approximately). The grammar is identical to the Napoleon and Hannibal maps. The difference is entirely in the data being encoded (wine volume vs. troop count) and the subject's stakes.

## The Core Question

If the Napoleon and Hannibal maps score in the 85-86 range, where should the wine map score? The technical execution (School Fidelity, Legibility, Execution) should be comparable. The data quality is high (contemporary commercial records). The difference must come from:

1. Purpose: commercial data has lower stakes than military tragedy
2. Resonance: wine trade generates different resonance than army attrition
3. Integrity: commercial data is contemporary and high-quality (no uncertainty penalty)
