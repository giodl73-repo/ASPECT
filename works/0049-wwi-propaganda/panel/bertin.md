# Bertin Review — 0049 WWI British Recruitment Propaganda

I will apply my visual variable analysis, because that is what I do — and then I will address the integrity problem, because I cannot ignore it.

Visual variable assessment: This is an illustration, not a chart. The visual variables are used illustratively, not encodings of quantitative or categorical data. There is no data to encode. The "encoding" is narrative: posture encodes emotional state (guilt = slumped and averted); the girl encodes futurity (children as representatives of future judgment); the toy soldiers encode the subject matter (military service). These are conventional illustrative associations, not Bertin-grammar visual variables.

The nearest data claim: the father's guilty posture implies that guilt is the appropriate response to civilian status. This is a normative claim encoded visually. In my grammar, this would be a case where an ordered variable (guilt/absence of guilt) is encoded through shape and posture. But the question of whether civilian status actually warrants guilt depends on facts (is the war just? are other men enlisting? is enlistment required?) that are not in the poster.

The ontological contamination: "fit civilian man who has not enlisted" is presented through the visual encoding as equivalent to "bad father, coward." The category is embedded as the natural reading of the father figure's visual encoding. There is no definition of this category; there is no argument for the equivalence; it is assumed and presented as the expected viewer identification.

This is, in my semiotic framework, a misuse of the associative property of visual variables. The illustration uses conventional associations (guilt posture = guilty man) to create a categorical identification that is then applied to the viewer. The viewer is supposed to see the father, recognize the guilty posture, identify with the father, and thereby feel guilty themselves. The chain of identification is the mechanism of coercion.

I find this work technically proficient and fundamentally dishonest. I will score accordingly.
