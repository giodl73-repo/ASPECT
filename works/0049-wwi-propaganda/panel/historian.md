# Historian Review — 0049 WWI British Recruitment Propaganda

Historical grounding is essential here.

The British Parliamentary Recruiting Committee produced over 12 million posters between 1914 and 1916. "Daddy, What Did YOU Do in the Great War?" (Savile Lumley, 1915) is among the most famous. The poster campaign was one of the largest propaganda operations in British history, and it achieved its goal: the British Expeditionary Force reached over two million volunteers by 1916.

The historical context: the poster was produced during the volunteer phase of British military recruitment (before conscription was introduced in January 1916). Its purpose was to fill the army before conscription became necessary. It represents the peak of guilt-based recruitment propaganda — a technique that was subsequently documented, analyzed, and criticized extensively.

Historical critics of the poster (even during WWI): some British writers, including portions of the suffragette movement, criticized the poster campaign for its psychological manipulation of working-class men who had little social or economic agency. The White Feather movement (women distributing white feathers to men not in uniform) was a parallel coercive mechanism. These methods were controversial at the time, not just in retrospect.

The historical question for DEGAS: can the rubric score a historically important work that is also a propaganda failure? Yes. Historical importance is not a rubric criterion. The work is historically important AND it is a manipulation designed to bypass rational assessment. Both are true.

School attribution: Advocacy-visualization (Type A). But the panel must grapple with whether advocacy-as-coercion is a legitimate form of advocacy-visualization. I argue it is a school-story mismatch: the advocacy-visualization school claims to persuade through evidence; this work coerces through guilt. The school grammar is superficially applied (it is a poster with a persuasive purpose) but the school's core claim (persuasion through evidence) is violated.

The Integrity failure is the most significant scoring issue. The poster implies a statistical reality that was contested in 1915 and has never been evidenced. More importantly, it is designed to prevent interrogation — the guilt loop is the encoding mechanism, not the argument. This is a genuine integrity failure.
