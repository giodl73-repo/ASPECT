# Audience Review — 0050 DEGAS Scoreboard

I want to be honest about what I understand and what I don't.

Looking at the chart: a sorted bar chart of 50 works. The bars go from the longest at the top (about 92.7 -- "vaccines") to the shortest at the bottom (about 57.1 -- "wwi-propaganda"). I understand this immediately. The longest bar got the highest score; the shortest bar got the lowest. The color encoding tells me the school -- blue is statistical-graphics, green is something ecology-related, brown is cartography. I need the legend to be sure which is which, and there are a lot of colors.

What I find interesting: I can see patterns. The top of the chart has mostly blue bars and green bars. The bottom has some red (advocacy?) and one very short bar. I don't need to know what "school" means to see that some colors cluster near the top and some don't appear at the top.

The work slug labels are small but I can read them if I lean in. I recognize some names: napoleon, nightingale, dubois. Others I don't recognize (haeckel, bertin). But the point of the chart isn't to recognize individual works -- it's to see the distribution. I get the distribution.

My question: what does the score mean? The chart shows me that vaccines scored 92.7 and propaganda scored 57.1. But why? The bar length tells me the score; it doesn't tell me which dimension was highest or lowest. For someone who hasn't read the individual reviews, the bar is a number without a reason.

This is a legitimate informational chart for an audience that knows the DEGAS project. For a general audience, it raises more questions than it answers -- which is appropriate for a project-internal documentation visualization.
