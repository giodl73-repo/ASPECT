# Playfair Review — 0050 DEGAS Scoreboard

This is a bar chart. A proper bar chart. Sorted descending by value, with the highest bars at the top, color coding a categorical variable, labels on both the left (identity) and right (value). I invented this form. I invented it so that comparison of ranked quantities would be immediate. This chart does exactly what I designed the form to do.

The subject — DEGAS works scored by a rubric — is exactly the kind of comparative question that bar charts answer: "which of these things ranks highest?" The school-story match is not merely adequate; it is perfect. The form serves the question without any residue.

Let me address the self-referential element. This chart includes work 0050 (this chart) as one of its 50 bars. This is a mild recursion: the chart is reviewed by the panel; the panel assigns a score; the score appears as bar 0050 in the chart; the chart depicts its own evaluation. I find this amusing rather than problematic. The recursion is declared; the data is complete; the chart does not pretend to be something other than what it is. A commercial trade atlas that included a page about the atlas itself would face the same observation, and I would make the same response: if the self-referential data is honest, include it.

The color encoding by school is the most important design decision. The DEGAS project has 14 schools; a categorical palette with 14 colors is near the limit of human color discrimination. I would use 5-6 major school groupings rather than 14 individual school colors. Statistical-graphics and its relatives (dynamic, advocacy) could share a blue family; ecological and scientific illustration could share a green family; cartography and flow-mapping could share a brown family. This would improve legibility without losing the categorical information.

The axis: I recommend a zero baseline with annotation "All works score above 55" rather than a truncated axis starting at 50. A truncated axis visually exaggerates the differences between works. The range 57-93 looks larger on a 50-100 scale than on a 0-100 scale. Since the argument is comparative ranking (not magnitude), the truncation is not unreasonable — but it should be declared with a zig-zag break symbol.
