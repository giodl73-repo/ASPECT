# Du Bois Review — 0051 Tobacco Physician Endorsement Campaigns

The number "20,679" is the whole of the argument. I want to examine exactly what kind of number this is.

When I brought charts to Paris in 1900, I displayed unflattering data accurately — literacy rates that showed our deprivation, income figures that showed our constraint. The precision was the point: these numbers can be checked, challenged, and argued with. That is what makes them an honest weapon.

"20,679 Physicians" cannot be checked. The American Tobacco Company selected this number, produced it through a process they controlled and never disclosed, and presented it with typographic authority borrowed from legitimate survey reporting. The number performs the role of data while refusing the obligations of data: source disclosure, methodology, verifiability. It is precision-as-forgery.

The mechanism is manufactured consensus. The company did not find that physicians endorsed their product; they created conditions designed to produce endorsement, then reported the count as if it were independent expert opinion. The audience — literate magazine readers in the 1930s — could not evaluate the claim because the evaluation apparatus was deliberately removed.

The rubric identifies manufactured consensus as a coercion mechanism: it removes the audience's ability to assess the claim by presenting controlled output as independent authority. This is a harder case than WWI propaganda (0049), which contained no data at all. Here there IS a number. The number is the coercion device.

**Rubric gap:** The Integrity questions cover suppressed uncertainty (Q1) and overstated causation (Q2), but do not specifically name manufactured consensus — data generated by the advocacy apparatus itself and presented as independent evidence. The distinction from ordinary suppression is important: the number is not merely imprecise; it is produced to appear independent while being entirely controlled. This warrants a named mechanism in any v2.3 amendment.
