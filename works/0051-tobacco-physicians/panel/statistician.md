# Statistician Review — 0051 Tobacco Physician Endorsement Campaigns

I will audit the "20,679 Physicians" claim as a statistical claim.

**What a legitimate physician survey would require:**
- Pre-registered question wording
- Defined sampling frame (which physicians, from what population)
- Blind administration (physicians unaware of sponsor)
- No material inducement (no free product prior to response)
- Outcome measure defined before data collection
- Confidence interval or response rate reported
- Funder disclosed

**What was actually done:**
- Physicians received complimentary Lucky Strike cigarettes
- Were then asked whether they found them "less irritating to the throat"
- Those who said yes were counted
- No control group; no comparison against baseline; no blind condition
- Funder not disclosed in the advertisement
- Response rate not reported
- Question wording not disclosed
- Survey instrument not available for review

The number 20,679 is real — that many physicians may indeed have answered yes to the question as posed. But the question as posed is not a health claim. It is a preference claim, post-exposure, from a biased sample (those who accepted free product) with a leading question, administered by or for the product's manufacturer. Presented as "physicians say LUCKIES are less irritating," the number constitutes a false statistical claim.

**The Frank Statement (1954):** Hill & Knowlton's campaign represents what I call source laundering — creating a new institutional source, funding it, directing its outputs, and then citing it as independent authority. This is methodologically distinct from ordinary suppressed uncertainty. The problem is not that uncertainty is suppressed; it is that the source of authority was itself manufactured.

**Rubric gap:** The rubric's Question 1 (disclosure of source quality) and Question 2 (argument integrity) cover suppression and overstatement. They do not have a named mechanism for source fabrication — where the evidence-generating apparatus is controlled by the advocacy party. I flag this as a gap requiring v2.3 treatment.
