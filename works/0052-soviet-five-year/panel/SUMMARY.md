## Scoring Pattern — School Fidelity/Execution Paradox

Work 0052 presents the Cluster X scoring paradox in its purest form: the highest School Fidelity (16.10/20) and Execution (13.35/15) scores in the cluster, combined with near-minimum Integrity. A technically excellent visualization of comprehensively false data. The rubric handles this correctly by separating the dimensions — the chart-making is scored independently of the data environment. But the paradox is worth naming for v2.3.

## Coercion Mechanism in Rubric Language

**Mechanism:** Captive source — data generated under conditions where reporters cannot report accurately without personal danger. Distinguished from manufactured consensus by: (a) the falsification is produced by incentive structure, not deliberate advocacy production; (b) the chart-maker may not know the data is false; (c) no methodology is withheld — the methodology may be correct; it is the inputs that are coerced.

**Rubric location:** Integrity Question 1 (source quality). The current rubric asks whether the work declares source data quality. A captive-source situation may not be declarable by the chart-maker. The rubric needs a pathway for: "what happens when the chart-maker cannot know, and the audience has no alternative information?"

**Purpose location:** 3.65/15. The state-level decision to use this visualization system functions to prevent citizens from knowing the state of the economy during a famine. This is a Purpose failure at the systemic level, not necessarily the individual level.

## Amendment Support

This work supports naming **captive source** as a distinct coercion mechanism:

> "Captive source: data generated under conditions where reporters cannot report accurately without personal danger to themselves or institutional survival. Distinguished from manufactured consensus by: the falsification emerges from coercive incentive structure rather than deliberate advocacy production. The chart-maker may be a victim of the same coercive system as the audience. Severity assessed by: degree of falsification, availability of alternative information to audience, and whether the coercive structure was designed to produce specific results."
