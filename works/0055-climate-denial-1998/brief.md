# Brief — 0055 Climate Denial Temperature Chart

## Historical Context

From approximately 2005 to 2015, climate denial advocacy organizations — prominently the Heartland Institute and the Global Warming Policy Foundation — circulated a family of charts showing global average temperature anomaly plotted from 1998 onward. The typical chart showed temperature data from 1998 to approximately 2012 or 2014, with a trend line showing approximately flat temperature or slight decline. The chart was titled or annotated with variants of "No Significant Warming Since 1998" or "The Warming Pause."

The strategy was explicit. The year 1998 was exceptional: a powerful El Niño event caused a spike in global average temperature that year, making it the warmest year in the record up to that point. Beginning the chart in 1998 meant beginning from a statistical outlier. The apparent "pause" was a mathematical consequence of starting from an unusually high point: any subsequent trajectory, even a genuinely warming one, would appear flat or declining relative to a local maximum.

## The Baseline Manipulation Mechanism

The coercion mechanism is **temporal baseline manipulation**: selecting a starting point that is a statistical outlier to produce a trend line that misrepresents the actual long-term pattern.

Key features:
- The data points are real: the temperature record is accurate
- The trend line is mathematically correct: it correctly represents the trend from 1998 onward
- The chart is visually standard: a legitimate time-series with a legitimate trend line
- The selection of 1998 as a start point is the entire argument: any other starting point (1970, 1975, 1980, 1985, 1990) produces a clearly upward trend
- The start-year selection is not disclosed as a choice: the chart presents as if 1998 were a natural or obvious starting point

The audience cannot evaluate this claim without knowing:
1. That 1998 was an El Niño outlier
2. That the trend from any earlier start point shows warming
3. That the "pause" disappears when 2014, 2015, and 2016 (also record-warm years) are included

None of this information is in the chart. The chart is technically accurate at every point and systematically misleading in its design.

## Documented Context

The "warming pause" narrative was a deliberate strategy developed in climate denial communications during this period, documented in materials exposed during the Heartland Institute document leak (2012) and in academic analyses of denial rhetoric (Lewandowsky et al., "The Pause in Global Warming," 2015; Foster and Rahmstorf, "Global temperature evolution 1979–2010," 2011). The specific choice of 1998 as a baseline was not a naive statistical decision; it was a communication strategy designed to produce a false impression of scientific ambiguity where scientific consensus was robust.
