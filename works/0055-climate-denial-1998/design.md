# Design Description — 0055 "No Warming Since 1998" Temperature Chart

## Visual Format

A standard time-series line chart — one of the most basic and widely understood statistical graphics formats. Line charts of temperature anomaly are among the most common forms in climate science communication; this chart deliberately mimics the form of legitimate scientific temperature records.

## Layout Architecture

**X-axis:** Years, 1998 to approximately 2012 or 2014. The axis begins precisely at the El Niño outlier year. The starting point is not annotated or explained.

**Y-axis:** Global average temperature anomaly (degrees Celsius relative to a baseline period). The scale is honest — values are not truncated or distorted.

**Data series:** Annual or monthly global average temperature values (from a real temperature dataset — HadCRUT, NASA GISS, or NOAA). The data points are real.

**Trend line:** A linear or smoothed trend line is fitted to the data from 1998 onward. Because 1998 is a local maximum, the trend line from that point is approximately flat, slightly downward, or weakly positive depending on the endpoint year.

**Title/annotation:** "No Significant Warming Since 1998" or "Global Warming Pause: 1998–[year]" in headline typography. The conclusion is stated above the chart as a finding.

## What Is Suppressed

No long-term context: the chart does not show data from before 1998. If it did, the El Niño spike would be visible as an outlier against a clearly rising trend.

No alternative start-year trend lines: a responsible representation of the trend question would show multiple trend lines (from 1970, 1980, 1990, 1998) to demonstrate that start-year choice drives the apparent result.

No confidence intervals on the trend line: the visual impression of a flat trend is not accompanied by uncertainty quantification.

No annotation of 1998 El Niño: the exceptional character of the starting point is not disclosed.

## Grammar Assessment

The time-series line chart grammar is fully correct. Position on y-axis encodes temperature; position on x-axis encodes time; the line connects sequential data points; the trend line estimates the central tendency. No variable is misapplied.

The coercion is in the temporal scope selection. The start date is a choice, and the choice is the entire argument. The chart's grammar presents this choice as if it were the obvious framing — as if anyone asking "is there warming?" would naturally begin in 1998. The grammar has no affordance for "this start date was selected to produce this result."
