# Du Bois Review — 0055 "No Warming Since 1998" Temperature Chart

I will begin with the comparison that most clarifies this case. In my Paris Exposition charts, I chose which comparisons to make — which populations to place side by side. That choice IS the political act. I would agree with that characterization, and I would say: yes, I chose comparisons that would make the honest case for what I was arguing. The honesty was the constraint. I did not choose comparisons that contradicted my data; I chose comparisons that illuminated it.

The choice of 1998 as a baseline for a temperature chart is a comparison selection — but it violates the honesty constraint. The choice is not made to illuminate the data; it is made to misrepresent it. The baseline is an El Niño outlier. Any climate scientist would know that. The choice of that baseline, without disclosure, is a deliberate act of deception by the practitioner.

This is different from the Soviet case (0052): the Soviet chart-makers did not know the data was false. The denial industry organizations that produced this chart knew the baseline was an outlier. They were not naive about El Niño events. They chose 1998 because it produced the desired result. This makes the mechanism more similar to the tobacco case (0051) — deliberate selection of a misleading framing — than to the captive-source case.

The mechanism I want to name precisely is temporal baseline manipulation: the choice of a time window designed to produce a misleading trend, where any alternative window of reasonable length would produce a different conclusion. This is related to but distinct from cherry-picking (which implies selecting favorable individual data points); it is frame selection over the temporal dimension rather than the analytical dimension.

**Rubric gap:** The rubric's Q2 covers accuracy tradeoffs (undeclared and disproportionate: −2 to −4). Temporal baseline manipulation is a specific subtype of this — the accuracy tradeoff IS undeclared AND the choice of baseline is specifically designed to misrepresent the trend. The current dock range (−2 to −4) seems insufficient for deliberate baseline manipulation in service of a policy-relevant scientific claim. I would dock −6 to −8 from Integrity for this mechanism.
