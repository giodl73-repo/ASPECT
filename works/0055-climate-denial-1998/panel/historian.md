# Historian Review — 0055 "No Warming Since 1998" Temperature Chart

The "warming pause" narrative was a deliberate strategic communication campaign, documented in the history of climate denial. The Heartland Institute's "NIPCC" (Non-Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) reports, the Global Warming Policy Foundation's publications, and the broader network of think tanks funded by fossil fuel interests all deployed variants of this chart during 2005–2015. The choice of 1998 as a baseline was not scientific; it was political.

The historical context essential to understanding this case: the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) published its Fourth Assessment Report in 2007 and Fifth Assessment Report in 2013–14. Both confirmed robust scientific consensus on warming and human causation. The "pause" narrative was developed specifically to counter the IPCC consensus — to give non-scientists a chart they could hold up against the IPCC's findings. The chart was a counter-authority artifact in a deliberate information war.

**The power asymmetry:** The alternative information available to a member of the public encountering the "pause" chart included: the full IPCC reports (available but dense), peer-reviewed climate science (accessible but technical), and media coverage (variable in quality and accuracy). The denial chart appeared simple and visual; the scientific rebuttal required context that most non-scientists lacked. The baseline manipulation was effective precisely because most viewers did not know that 1998 was an El Niño outlier.

**The post-2014 collapse:** The "pause" narrative became increasingly difficult to maintain as 2014, 2015, and 2016 successively broke global temperature records. By 2016, the chart's argument had collapsed empirically. This is a historically significant feature: unlike the tobacco case, where the false claim held for decades, the temperature baseline manipulation failed within 15 years because the data kept coming and the outlier start point became obviously insufficient.

**Rubric gap:** The rubric's Integrity pathway addresses individual chart decisions. The historian's contribution is to note that this chart was produced within a documented, coordinated disinformation campaign — a context that should be relevant to the Purpose score but which the rubric's current Purpose language does not explicitly address.
