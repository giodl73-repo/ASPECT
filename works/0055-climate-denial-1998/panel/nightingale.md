# Nightingale Review — 0055 "No Warming Since 1998" Temperature Chart

I designed the coxcomb diagram to reach people who would not act on tables and statistics. I made deliberate choices about presentation — choices that made the argument clearer than a bar chart or table would have. I chose the form for advocacy purposes. The form served an honest argument.

The question for this chart is: does the presentation choice serve an honest argument or misrepresent the data? And the answer, when you examine the 1998 baseline, is unambiguous. The choice of 1998 as a starting point is the argument. There is no scientific or methodological reason to start a temperature trend analysis in 1998. It is an El Niño year — climatologists know this and account for it. The only reason to start in 1998 is to produce the appearance of a pause in a record that, from any other starting point, shows warming.

This chart represents a class of deception I find particularly troubling: it uses the visual grammar of legitimate scientific reporting to produce a false impression of scientific consensus. When I see a time-series temperature chart, I expect it to have been produced by scientists following the conventions of their field — which include not selecting outlier start dates to manufacture results. This chart wears the costume of those conventions while violating their substance.

The policy consequences are the Purpose failure. Climate action requires public understanding that warming is occurring. This chart was designed and distributed specifically to undermine that understanding — to give politicians and members of the public a chart that appeared to show scientific evidence of a "pause" they could cite to delay action. The consequences of successful climate inaction are measured in human suffering. The Purpose failure here is comparable to the tobacco case: preventing a population from correctly assessing a health risk.
