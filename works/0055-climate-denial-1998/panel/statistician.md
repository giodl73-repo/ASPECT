# Statistician Review — 0055 "No Warming Since 1998" Temperature Chart

I will audit the temporal baseline selection with statistical precision, because this case is purely a statistical fraud in the sense that I care about: the manipulation is entirely in a methodological choice.

**The El Niño outlier problem.** The year 1998 recorded a global average temperature anomaly of approximately +0.61°C above the 1951–1980 baseline (NASA GISS). The years 1996 and 1997 recorded +0.33°C and +0.46°C respectively. The jump from 1997 to 1998 — approximately +0.15°C in one year — is attributable to the 1997–98 El Niño event, one of the strongest in the instrumental record. Beginning a trend analysis from a local maximum produced by a known anomalous event and calling the result "no warming" is equivalent to measuring a person's weight after a large meal and concluding "no weight loss" compared to their weight at the meal's peak.

**The sensitivity to start year.** Foster and Rahmstorf (2011) demonstrated this directly: the apparent "pause" is an artifact of start-year selection. Their analysis, adjusting for ENSO (El Niño–Southern Oscillation), volcanic forcing, and solar output, showed a clear warming signal from 1979–2010 with no pause. The HadCRUT4 dataset from 1970 to 2014 shows a warming trend of approximately +0.18°C per decade. The apparent pause from 1998 is entirely explained by start-year selection combined with the El Niño outlier.

**The trend line analysis.** A linear trend fitted to 1998–2012 data would show slope of approximately −0.01 to +0.02°C per decade — statistically indistinguishable from zero. A linear trend fitted to 1970–2012 data shows +0.17°C per decade. The apparent "pause" is a window effect, not a signal.

**Statistical audit finding:** The chart commits a named statistical error — regression to the mean starting from an outlier — and does not disclose it. The correct statistical practice for assessing temperature trends is to use a long time series (decades), control for known oscillation effects (ENSO), and test for structural breaks. This chart does none of these. The fraud is in the methodological choice; the implementation of that choice is technically correct.
